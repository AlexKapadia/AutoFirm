# BEST-PARTS — McKinsey B2B Pulse

## ADOPT
- **Adopt omnichannel + rule-of-thirds as AutoFirm's channel-orchestration default for B2B.** The sales agent must be able to engage across in-person-equivalent (booked human meetings), remote-human (video/chat), and digital self-serve — and offer all three rather than forcing one. *Build implication:* L2.B8 channel-routing component exposes ≥3 channel modes; the agent picks per stakeholder preference signal, defaulting to "offer choice."
- **Adopt self-serve-capable large-deal handling.** Because buyers will close large deals digitally, AutoFirm should NOT assume "big deal ⇒ must be human-led." This justifies the hybrid product-led-sales motion (source 10) as a first-class option.
- **Adopt the rule-of-thirds invariance as a generality anchor:** McKinsey explicitly reports it holds across industry/geo/size/value — a strong, cited basis for a *single* channel framework that generalizes across the entire B12 panel.

## REJECT
- **Reject single-channel sales designs** (rep-only OR self-serve-only). The evidence says winners run ~10 channels; a one-channel motion is an instant under-performer.
- **Reject consultancy framing uncritically** — cite the *survey numbers* (rule of thirds, ~10 channels), not McKinsey's prescriptive narrative, and corroborate with Gartner (source 06).

## Why this matters for generality
The rule of thirds is McKinsey's own cross-industry/cross-size/cross-value generalization — the strongest single piece of cited evidence that ONE channel framework can serve the whole B12 "any company" panel, which is exactly AutoFirm's mandate.
