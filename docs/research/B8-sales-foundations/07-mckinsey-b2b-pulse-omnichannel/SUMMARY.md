## What the source actually says (faithful summary)
- **The "Rule of Thirds" (the headline finding):** at any given stage of the buying journey, customers split roughly evenly across three modes — **~one-third want in-person (traditional) interactions, ~one-third want remote human (e.g. video/phone), and ~one-third want digital self-serve.** McKinsey reports this "holds true across all geographies, industries, and company sizes... all types of buying occasions from new to repeat purchases, and across high- and low-value purchases."
- **Omnichannel is now mandatory:** B2B customers use an **average of ~10 interaction channels** in their buying journey, up from ~5 in 2016. "B2B winners" uniformly adhere to the rule of thirds and use ~10 channels.
- **Buying group size:** consistent with Gartner — buying committees of **~6–10 decision-makers**, each researching independently.
- **Hybrid sellers + e-commerce + self-serve** are all rising simultaneously; buyers will spend large sums via digital self-serve (McKinsey reports willingness to spend $50k+ — and a meaningful share even $500k+ — through fully self-serve or remote channels).
