## What the source actually says (faithful summary)
Defines the modern go-to-market **motion** axis — how a company acquires/activates/monetizes:

- **Product-Led Growth (PLG):** "the product itself acquires, activates, engages, monetizes, and retains users... most commonly without human touch." Users discover via free trial/freemium, experience value self-serve, and upgrade without a rep. Acquisition is cheap/scalable; relies on a **Product-Qualified Lead (PQL)** — a user who has hit an in-product value/usage threshold — as the conversion signal (the PLG analogue of the MQL/SQL).
- **Sales-Led Growth (SLG):** "a dedicated sales team identifies, qualifies, and converts leads through personalized outreach"; the traditional enterprise model — a rep sells over a long cycle, typically to a senior executive. Higher cost per deal, higher ACV, supports complex/regulated buys.
- **Hybrid — Product-Led Sales (PLS):** "combines elements of PLG with the more traditional enterprise model of sales-led growth." Self-serve drives bottom-up adoption and generates PQLs; sales reps then engage the best self-serve accounts to expand into larger contracts (land-and-expand). McKinsey: the most competitive B2B SaaS run hybrid motions.

## Key claims AutoFirm can rely on
1. There is a **spectrum of motions** — PLG (no-touch) ↔ PLS (hybrid) ↔ SLG (high-touch) — selectable by product/ACV/buyer. Locator: McKinsey 2023; Bush 2019.
2. PLG's conversion signal is the **PQL (in-product behaviour threshold)**, distinct from the human-qualified MQL/SQL. Locator: PLG literature.
3. Hybrid (PLS) is the modern frontier and corroborates McKinsey's "buyers self-serve large deals" finding (source 07). Locator: McKinsey PLS article.

## Reproducibility note
The PLG/SLG/PLS definitions are consistent across Bush (2019), OpenView, Reforge, and McKinsey (2023) — independent sources agreeing on the taxonomy. The PQL concept is standard across all PLG sources.
