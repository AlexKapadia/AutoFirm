## What the source actually says (faithful summary)
AIDA = **Attention, Interest, Desire, Action** — the stage sequence visualised as the inverted "purchase funnel". This paper is a revisionist history of its creation.

- **Dominant (per this paper, incorrect) account:** Strong (1925) wrote: "E. St. Elmo Lewis formulated the slogan, 'Attract attention, maintain interest, create desire,' in 1898. Later he added the fourth term, 'get action'" (Strong, 1925a, p.76). This became the textbook origin story for over a century.
- **Iwamoto's finding (exact, abstract):** "The dominant hypothesis that E. St. Elmo Lewis invented and formulated AIDA is unsupported or based on weak evidence. There is no evidence indicating that Lewis invented the prototype of AIDA in or around 1898."
- **Corrected attribution (exact):** "Arthur Frederick Sheldon recognized the importance of the fourth word and the necessity of AIDA's theorization much earlier than Lewis... Sheldon — not Lewis — can be regarded as the originator of AIDA. Frank H. Dukesmith and Arthur Frederick Sheldon — not Lewis — contributed decisively to the formulation of AIDA."
- **Model lineage:** AIDA is "probably the most widely quoted model in the world of marketing, advertising, and selling" and seeded the hierarchy-of-effects family — Lavidge & Steiner (1961), Colley/DAGMAR (1961), McGuire (1978) — plus later extensions (AISDALSL etc.). "One of the longest serving hierarchical models."

## Key claims AutoFirm can rely on
