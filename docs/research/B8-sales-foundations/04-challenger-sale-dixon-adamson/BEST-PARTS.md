# BEST-PARTS — The Challenger Sale (Dixon & Adamson, 2011)

## ADOPT
- **Adopt Teach–Tailor–Take-Control as the OUTBOUND value-message engine for complex B2B.** AutoFirm agents are uniquely well-suited to the "Teach" step: they can synthesise commercial insight from public data (a prospect's filings, industry benchmarks) into a reframe the buyer hasn't considered. *Build implication:* L2.B8 ships an `insight_brief_generator` that, from public firmographic + industry data, produces a tailored "teaching" point per stakeholder — the Challenger "commercial insight" — feeding the SPIN discovery (source 03) and MEDDIC Champion enablement (source 08).
- **Adopt the empirical de-prioritisation of pure relationship-building** in complex deals (7% star rate). The autonomous agent should weight *insight delivery* over *rapport-only* activities when scoring next-best-action for high-complexity opportunities.
- **Adopt "Tailor" as stakeholder-level personalization** — wire to the buying-center roles model (source 05): a different tailored message per DMU role.

## REJECT
- **Reject Challenger as the SMB/transactional or self-serve motion.** The study's effect is specific to complex, multi-stakeholder B2B; in PLG/self-serve (source 10) there may be no rep to "take control." Methodology selection by motion is mandatory.
- **Reject "Take Control" as aggression.** The evidence supports *constructive* control (talking money, pushing back with insight), not pressure — which source 03 shows backfires. The agent's guardrails must keep this distinction.

## Why this matters for generality
Teach/Tailor/Take-Control is industry-neutral: the *insight* content is industry-specific, the *behaviour pattern* is constant — exactly the parameterize-don't-overfit pattern the B12 panel demands.
