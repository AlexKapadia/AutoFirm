## What the source actually says (faithful summary)
CEB studied **~6,000 sales representatives across ~90 companies** and classified reps into **five profiles** by behaviour: **Hard Worker, Relationship Builder, Lone Wolf, Reactive Problem Solver, and Challenger.**

- **Headline finding:** the **Challenger** profile dramatically outperforms the others in complex B2B sales. Of all high performers in the study, **nearly 40% were Challengers**, whereas **Relationship Builders produced the fewest star performers — just 7%**. (Exact figures as reported by the authors.)
- **The counter-intuitive result:** the **Relationship Builder** — long assumed to be the ideal B2B seller — is the *worst* performer among high-complexity reps. Relationship-building is necessary but not sufficient.
- **The Challenger model — three behaviours (Teach, Tailor, Take Control):**
  - **Teach** the customer something new/valuable about their own business (commercial insight / reframe).
  - **Tailor** the message to the specific stakeholder's economic drivers and concerns.
  - **Take Control** of the conversation, including talking about money and pushing back constructively (not aggressively).
- **Context:** research began during the Great Recession, when sales cycles elongated and buying anxiety rose — conditions that rewarded insight-led selling.

## Key claims AutoFirm can rely on
1. In complex B2B, **insight-led "teaching" selling > pure relationship-building** (40% vs 7% of star performers). Locator: Chapter on the five profiles.
2. The winning behaviour decomposes into Teach / Tailor / Take Control — an automatable behaviour set.
