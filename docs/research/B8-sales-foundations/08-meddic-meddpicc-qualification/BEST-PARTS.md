# BEST-PARTS — MEDDIC / MEDDPICC

## ADOPT
- **Adopt MEDDPICC as AutoFirm's deterministic deal-QUALIFICATION contract for complex B2B.** Its 8 fields are an ideal machine-checkable schema: each is a slot the agent must fill from evidence before advancing/forecasting a deal. This gives the autonomous sales agent a *fail-closed qualification gate* — a deal cannot be marked "commit/forecast" unless Economic Buyer, Metrics, Decision Process, Champion, and Paper Process are evidenced. *Build implication:* `DealQualification` typed contract (8 fields, each with evidence + confidence) in L2.B8; forecasting/auto-advance logic reads it; missing critical fields → the deal is flagged, not advanced (fail-closed, per CLAUDE §5.6).
- **Adopt MEDDPICC as the integration spine across methodologies:** Identify-Pain ← SPIN discovery (source 03); Metrics/Economic-Buyer ← Challenger Teach + buying-center (sources 04, 05); Decision-Process/Paper-Process ← Gartner buying jobs (source 06: requirements/validation/consensus). One contract unifies the playbook.

## REJECT
- **Reject MEDDPICC for transactional/B2C/self-serve sales.** It is explicitly a *complex-deal* qualification tool; applying its 8-field overhead to a $20/mo self-serve signup is pure overfitting. Gate its use behind buy-class = New Task / high deal value.
- **Reject treating MEDDIC as an efficacy claim.** It has no controlled study; adopt the *structure*, prove efficacy via AutoFirm's own evidence (win-rate of well-qualified vs poorly-qualified deals on the golden set).

## Why this matters for generality
The 8 fields are industry-invariant (every complex purchase has an economic buyer, decision process, and paper process); only the *content* differs across the B12 panel. The schema parameterizes cleanly; it does not overfit.
