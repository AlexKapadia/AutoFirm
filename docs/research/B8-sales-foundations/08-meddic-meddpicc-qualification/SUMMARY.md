# SUMMARY — MEDDIC / MEDDPICC (the dominant B2B deal-qualification framework)

## Full citation
- **Origin:** MEDDIC was created in ~1996 by Dick Dunkel, Jack Napoli, and colleagues at **PTC (Parametric Technology Corp.)**; credited with helping PTC scale from ~$300M to ~$1B revenue.
- **Authoritative reference:** MEDDIC Academy (Dunkel/Napoli lineage); Force Management (Command of the Message / MEDDICC); Andy Whyte, *MEDDICC* (2020).
- **URLs:** https://meddic.academy/meddic-sales-methodology-checklist/ ; https://www.forcemanagement.com/blog/meddic-vs.-meddpic-the-meaning-difference-and-benefits-of-each-for-sales-qualification
- **Type:** Practitioner/industry framework (widely adopted; not peer-reviewed).

## Questions informed
- **L1.B8.1** — deal qualification: how complex B2B deals are scored/advanced; the funnel's "is this real?" gate.

## GRADE tier
- **Low (practitioner).** Not peer-reviewed; no controlled efficacy study. ## What the source actually says (faithful summary)
MEDDIC is a **qualification checklist** for complex B2B opportunities — answering "should we invest in this deal, and what's missing to win it?" The acronym (and its extensions):

- **M — Metrics:** the quantified economic value/ROI the buyer will gain ("the economic benefit").
- **E — Economic Buyer:** the single person with discretionary control over the funds / the PO.
- **D — Decision Criteria:** the (technical, financial, vendor) criteria the buyer will use to choose.
- **D — Decision Process:** the steps/timeline/approvals by which the buyer will actually decide.
- **I — Identify Pain:** the concrete business pain driving the purchase (maps to SPIN's "explicit need").
- **C — Champion:** an internal advocate with influence who sells on your behalf.
- **Extensions:**
  - **MEDDIC + C = MEDDICC** adds **Competition** (who/what else you're up against, including "do nothing").
  - **MEDDPICC** adds **P — Paper Process** (procurement/legal/security review steps) → 8 components: Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Paper Process, Identify Pain, Champion, Competition.

## Key claims AutoFirm can rely on
1. MEDDPICC is an 8-field **structured qualification contract** that decomposes "is this deal winnable" into individually verifiable elements. Locator: MEDDIC Academy checklist; Force Management.
2. It maps cleanly onto the buying-center (source 05: Economic Buyer = Decider/Buyer; Champion = Influencer) and onto SPIN/Challenger (Identify Pain = SPIN explicit need; Metrics = Challenger "Teach" value case).

## Reproducibility note
The 8 components are stated verbatim and identically across MEDDIC Academy, Force Management, and Andy Whyte's *MEDDICC* (2020). The framework is a taxonomy (definitional), so verification is by cross-source agreement on the component list — which is unanimous.
