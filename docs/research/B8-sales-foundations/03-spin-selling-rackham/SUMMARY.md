## What the source actually says (faithful summary)
SPIN is the result of the Huthwaite corporation's **12-year, ~$1 million research program** analysing **~35,000 sales calls** across many countries/companies, focused specifically on the **large/complex sale**.

- **Core finding (the empirical part):** behaviours that work in small sales FAIL in large ones. Specifically, classic **closing techniques and conventional "objection handling"** correlate with success in small sales but with *failure* in large sales — "trying to close too early alienates professional buyers."
- **The SPIN question sequence** (what high performers actually do — ask questions in this progression):
  - **S — Situation** questions: establish context/facts about the buyer's current state.
  - **P — Problem** questions: uncover difficulties, dissatisfactions, problems ("implied needs").
  - **I — Implication** questions: develop the seriousness/consequences of those problems (make them feel urgent/costly).
  - **N — Need-payoff** questions: get the buyer to articulate the value/benefit of solving them ("explicit needs").
- **Mechanism:** success in large sales comes from converting *implied needs* into *explicit needs* via Implication and Need-payoff questions, then demonstrating capability — NOT from pressure/closing. Skilled sellers "explore customer needs in depth, demonstrate capability to meet those needs, and show a clear business case before they attempt to close."
