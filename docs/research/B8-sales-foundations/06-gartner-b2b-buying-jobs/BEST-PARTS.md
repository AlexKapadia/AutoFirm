# BEST-PARTS — Gartner Six Buying Jobs

## ADOPT
- **Adopt the six buying-jobs as AutoFirm's B2B opportunity STATE MODEL (parallel, not sequential).** Instead of a single funnel-stage index, each opportunity tracks completion/confidence on all six jobs simultaneously (problem-ID, solution-exploration, requirements, supplier-selection, validation, consensus). The agent's next-best-action targets the *least-complete, highest-leverage* job — not "the next stage." *Build implication:* `BuyingJobsState` (6 scored dimensions) on the L2.B8 opportunity record; replaces naive linear stage gating while keeping the funnel stage (source 01) for CRM reporting.
- **Adopt "buyer enablement" as a first-class agent capability.** Since sales gets only ~17% of buyer time and 61–67% prefer rep-free, the highest-leverage sales activity is producing **self-serve enablement content** (comparison tools, ROI calculators, validation assets) that does the selling when no rep is present. *Build implication:* the sales agent generates buyer-enablement artifacts (tied to B15 artifact generation), not just rep talk tracks.
- **Adopt "consensus creation" as an explicit job the agent works** — multi-threading the DMU (source 05) to manufacture buying-group agreement, the hardest of the six jobs.

## REJECT
- **Reject linear-only pipeline logic for B2B.** The looping evidence makes strict stage-gating a misrepresentation of the buyer. Keep the funnel for reporting; drive action from the six-jobs state.
- **Reject rep-centric assumptions** — design for a journey where the buyer self-serves most of the way.

## Why this matters for generality
The six jobs are deliberately abstract (problem→solution→requirements→select→validate→consensus) and apply to ANY considered B2B purchase across the B12 panel; the *content* per job is industry-specific. Generality by parameterization.
