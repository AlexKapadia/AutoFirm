## What the source actually says (faithful summary)
Gartner reframes B2B buying from a linear funnel into **six "buying jobs"** that a buying group must complete — but **in no fixed order**, with constant looping:

1. **Problem identification** — "We need to do something."
2. **Solution exploration** — "What's out there to solve our problem?"
3. **Requirements building** — "What exactly do we need the purchase to do?"
4. **Supplier selection** — "Does this do what we want it to do?"
5. **Validation** — "We think we know the right answer, but we need to be sure."
6. **Consensus creation** — "We need to get everyone on board."

- **Non-linearity (exact):** B2B buying "doesn't play out in any kind of predictable, linear order." Buyers "loop" — revisiting each of the six jobs "at least once" across a typical purchase. This is the empirical refutation of the strict funnel (corroborating source 02).
- **Key statistics (as Gartner reports them):**
  - B2B buyers spend only **~17%** of their total purchase time meeting with potential suppliers; across multiple suppliers, any single supplier may get only **~5–6%** of buying time.
  - The **average B2B buying group = 6–10 decision makers**, each with ~4–5 independent pieces of research (corroborates source 07).
  - CEB (2012/2015): buyers were on average **~57%** through the purchase decision before engaging a supplier sales rep.
  - **61–67%** of B2B buyers (2025–2026 surveys) say they would prefer a rep-free / self-serve buying experience for at least part of the journey.
