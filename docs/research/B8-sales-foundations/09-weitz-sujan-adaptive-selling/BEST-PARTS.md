# BEST-PARTS — Weitz, Sujan & Sujan (1986) Adaptive Selling

## ADOPT
- **Adopt adaptive selling as the THEORETICAL spine and the SELECTION ENGINE of AutoFirm's sales playbook.** This is the academic justification for everything else: don't run one fixed motion — *adapt the approach to the situation*. AutoFirm operationalizes the paper's "if customer-type X → strategy Y" contingency knowledge as an explicit, auditable **methodology-selection rule base**: (buy-class, deal value, DMU complexity, channel preference) → {self-serve | SPIN discovery | Challenger insight | MEDDPICC qualification}. *Build implication:* a deterministic `MethodologySelector` in L2.B8 — this is the literature-grounded answer to "which methodology when," and it is glass-box/auditable (CLAUDE §3.5 deterministic guardrails).
- **Adopt ADAPTS as a literature-grounded efficacy metric** for AutoFirm's own evidence: measure whether the agent actually adapts (varies behaviour by situation) vs. runs a canned script — feeding the `evidence/` showcase (CLAUDE §3.10).
- **Adopt the knowledge-structures insight:** the agent's quality scales with the richness/organization of its customer-type → strategy mapping — argues for memory/learning (A4) over the sales function.

## REJECT
- **Reject the raw 16-item ADAPTS scale as-is** — the literature (incl. its own authors) found it not cleanly unidimensional; use a refined/shortened validated version or a behaviourally-grounded proxy, and note the caveat.
- **Reject one-size-fits-all scripts** outright — the paper's whole finding is that fixed behaviour underperforms situational adaptation.

## Why this matters for generality
Adaptive selling is the MOST general principle in B8: it says the right behaviour is *a function of the situation*, which is precisely the parameterize-by-context mandate of the B12 panel. It elevates AutoFirm's sales engine from "pick a methodology" to "select the methodology that fits THIS situation" — the generality requirement, expressed as theory.
