# SUMMARY — Channels: The Paid / Owned / Earned (POE) Media Framework

**Question(s) informed:** L1.B7.1 (marketing channels — the structural taxonomy of media/distribution channels).

## Full citations
- **Primary origin (analyst):** Goodall, D. (2009). "Owned, bought and earned media." (Nokia/Sarah Burnett-era analyst post; one of the first POE articulations.) Popularized concurrently by **Forrester Research** (Sean Corcoran, 2009, "Defining Earned, Owned and Paid Media," Forrester blog).
- **Practitioner reference (corroborating):** Smart Insights, "The difference between paid, owned and earned media." https://www.smartinsights.com/digital-marketing-strategy/customer-acquisition-strategy/new-media-options/
- **Academic context (channels theory, primary):** Coelho, P.S. & Easingwood, C. — multichannel distribution literature; and the integrated-marketing-communications (IMC) tradition (Schultz, D.E., Tannenbaum & Lauterborn, 1993, *Integrated Marketing Communications*, NTC).

## GRADE tier
- POE framework: **Low->Moderate** (industry/analyst origin — Forrester; widely adopted but not peer-reviewed). Used as the **taxonomy of record** for channel structure; corroborated by multiple independent industry sources.
- IMC tradition (Schultz et al.): **Moderate** (recognized academic/practitioner body of work) — corroborates the integration principle.

## Core content (faithful summary)
Marketing channels are structured into three controllable/influenceable types:
1. **Paid media** — channels the firm pays for visibility: search/social/display ads, sponsorships, paid influencers, affiliates. **Pro:** fast, scalable, controllable; **con:** stops when spend stops; least trusted (audiences know it's the brand).
2. **Owned media** — channels the firm controls: website, blog, email list, app, branded social profiles. **Pro:** control, low marginal cost, durable; **con:** slow to build reach.
3. **Earned media** — exposure others give the brand: PR, press, reviews, word-of-mouth, organic shares, UGC. **Pro:** most trusted, high credibility; **con:** not directly controllable.

**Integration principle (IMC):** the three reinforce each other — a coordinated mix outperforms siloed channels ("multiplier effect"); paid drives reach, owned converts/retains, earned builds trust. Extensions exist (e.g. "POGLE" adding Granted/Leased; "Shared" media) but POE is the durable core.

## Key claims (locators)
1. Channels partition into **Paid / Owned / Earned** by who controls the exposure (Forrester/Corcoran 2009; corroborated Smart Insights).
2. **Earned media is more trusted than paid** because the source is independent of the brand (multiple independent industry sources).
3. **Integrated (cross-channel) execution has a multiplier effect** vs. siloed channels (IMC tradition, Schultz et al. 1993).
4. POE is a *control/trust* taxonomy, NOT a measurement model — it does not assign conversion credit (that is attribution, folders 05-08).

## Reproducibility note
POE taxonomy is the de facto industry standard; reproducible/verifiable across many independent industry references. The integration "multiplier" claim is qualitative in the practitioner literature and should be quantified per-client via MMM/experiments (folders 07/08), not asserted as a fixed number.
