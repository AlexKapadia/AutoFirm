# BEST-PARTS — Binet & Field

## ADOPT
- **The brand/activation dual-mechanism model** as AutoFirm's framing of "marketing spend." Every plan splits into long-term brand-building and short-term activation, measured on DIFFERENT time windows. **Build implication:** the budget engine carries two pools with distinct KPIs and measurement cadences (brand: penetration/awareness/share over quarters; activation: conversions/CAC over days-weeks).
- **~60:40 as a PRIOR, not a constant.** **Build implication (critical for generality):** the playbook seeds the split at ~60:40 then *adjusts by industry/stage* — e.g. activation-heavier for early-stage startups needing immediate revenue, B2B considered purchases, or online-only DTC; brand-heavier for scaled consumer brands. The split is an industry-parameterized function, NEVER a hard-coded 60/40 magic constant (CLAUDE.md 3.9 anti-overfit). An adversarial test asserts the engine produces a *different, justified* split for each panel row.
- **Anti-short-termism guardrail.** **Build implication:** because last-click ROI (folders 05/07) biases toward activation, a governance check warns when a plan starves brand-building below an evidence-based floor for that industry — preventing the documented failure mode.

## REJECT
- **Reject 60:40 as a universal rule** — explicitly contested for B2B/online/early-stage; treating it as fixed would overfit FMCG award data to all clients. Adopt the mechanism + the prior, not the number.
- Reject using a single short-window metric to judge brand spend (it will always look like it "failed" because brand effects decay over years).

## Why
Gives AutoFirm an evidence-backed, industry-parameterized way to split and measure brand vs. performance budget, with built-in protection against the field's most common and costly error (short-termism), while staying general across the industry panel.
