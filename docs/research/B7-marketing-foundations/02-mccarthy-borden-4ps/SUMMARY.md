# SUMMARY — McCarthy (1960) & Borden (1964): The Marketing Mix / 4Ps

**Question(s) informed:** L1.B7.1 (the 4Ps marketing mix — the core controllable tactical levers).

## Full citations
- **Title:** Basic Marketing: A Managerial Approach — **Author:** E. Jerome McCarthy — **Year:** 1960 — **Venue:** Richard D. Irwin, Homewood, IL (textbook; primary). The book that distilled the mix into the **4Ps: Product, Price, Place, Promotion**.
- **Title:** The Concept of the Marketing Mix — **Author:** Neil H. Borden — **Year:** 1964 — **Venue:** *Journal of Advertising Research*, Vol. 4, pp. 2-7. Borden's account of the mix and its 12 original elements; he credits James Culliton (1948) for the "mixer of ingredients" metaphor.
- Origin pointer: Culliton, J. (1948) described the marketing executive as a "mixer of ingredients."

## GRADE tier
- McCarthy 1960: **Low->Moderate** as a source TIER (practitioner/academic textbook, not peer-reviewed venue) but it is the **primary** origin of the 4Ps and is universally attributed; treated as the citation of record for the 4P formulation.
- Borden 1964: **Moderate** — published in *Journal of Advertising Research* (peer-reviewed industry journal); primary author of "marketing mix" term.
- Corroborated by the secondary review: Goi, C.L. (2009) "A Review of Marketing Mix: 4Ps or More?" *International Journal of Marketing Studies* 1(1) — used only to corroborate history, not as a primary basis.

## Core content (faithful summary)
- **Borden** (popularizing Culliton's metaphor) framed marketing as blending **ingredients** the manager controls. His original list had **12 elements**: product planning, pricing, branding, channels of distribution, personal selling, advertising, promotions, packaging, display, servicing, physical handling, fact-finding/analysis.
- **McCarthy (1960)** simplified Borden's 12 into **four controllable categories — the 4Ps**:
  1. **Product** — the offering: features, quality, branding, packaging, service, assortment.
  2. **Price** — list price, discounts, allowances, payment/credit terms.
  3. **Place** (distribution) — channels, coverage, locations, inventory, logistics.
  4. **Promotion** — advertising, personal selling, sales promotion, publicity/PR.
- The 4Ps are the **firm-controllable tactical levers** aimed at a chosen target market (linking to STP).

## Key claims (locators)
1. The marketing mix = the set of *controllable* variables the firm blends to produce a response in the target market (Borden 1964, pp. 2-7).
2. The mix reduces to **4 categories** (McCarthy 1960).
3. Extensions exist: **7Ps** (Booms & Bitner 1981 added People, Process, Physical Evidence for services); **4Cs** (Lauterborn 1990: Customer value, Cost, Convenience, Communication) — a customer-centric reframing.

## Reproducibility note
4P taxonomy reproducible from any edition of McCarthy's *Basic Marketing*; history corroborated by Goi (2009) and Borden (1964). Service-extension (7Ps) traces to Booms & Bitner (1981), *Marketing of Services*, AMA.
