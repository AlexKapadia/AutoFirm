# BEST-PARTS — McCarthy/Borden 4Ps

## ADOPT
- **4Ps as the canonical tactical-lever schema** for AutoFirm's per-segment marketing decision. After STP selects a target, the playbook emits a **4P decision object**: { product, price, place, promotion } with each sub-field parameterized. **Build implication:** typed `MarketingMix` contract; Price links to L1.B5.1 (pricing) and Place to L1.B11.1 (ops/distribution) — clean cross-branch contracts, no duplication.
- **7Ps switch for services/regulated industries.** Because AutoFirm must generalize across the fixed industry panel (incl. professional services, healthcare, restaurants), adopt the **7P extension** (add People, Process, Physical Evidence) as a *conditional* expansion when the business is service-dominant. **Build implication:** mix schema is industry-parameterized — physical-goods clients use 4Ps; service clients expand to 7Ps. Directly satisfies the generalization gate (panel rows 2,7,8).
- **4Cs as a customer-centric review lens** (Lauterborn 1990) — used by a QA/critique agent to sanity-check that each "P" was set from the customer's view (value/cost/convenience/communication), not the firm's.

## REJECT
- **Reject the 4Ps as a complete strategy.** They are *tactics* downstream of STP and channel/measurement strategy; treating them as the whole plan is the classic error. AutoFirm sequences STP -> channels -> mix -> measurement.
- Reject endless P-proliferation (5Es, 7Cs, etc.) as primary structure — adopt only the well-established 4P/7P/4C set to avoid overfitting to one trendy framework.

## Why
The 4Ps give a stable, industry-general, controllable-lever contract that every downstream playbook stage can populate, while the 7P switch makes it provably general across the service/physical panel.
