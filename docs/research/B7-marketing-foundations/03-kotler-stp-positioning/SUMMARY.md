## Core content (faithful summary)
Kotler formalized **STP** as the sequential core of strategic marketing — widely quoted as: "the essence of strategic marketing can be captured in three words: segmentation, targeting, positioning (STP)."

1. **Segmentation** — divide the market into distinct buyer groups with different needs/behaviors. Common **bases**: geographic, demographic, psychographic, behavioral.
2. **Targeting** — evaluate segments on attractiveness and fit, then select which to serve. Evaluation criteria: segment **size & growth**, **structural attractiveness** (competition, substitutes), and **company objectives/resources**. Target strategies: undifferentiated (mass), differentiated, concentrated (niche), micromarketing.
3. **Positioning** — design the offer and image to occupy a distinctive, valued place in target customers' minds relative to competitors; expressed via a **positioning statement** and a **value proposition** / points-of-difference vs. points-of-parity.

## Key claims (locators)
1. STP is a **sequence**: you cannot position before you have targeted, nor target before you have segmented (Marketing Management, STP chapters).
2. Segmentation requires segments to be **measurable, substantial, accessible, differentiable, actionable** (the classic effectiveness criteria for a useful segmentation).
3. Positioning is **relative and perceptual** — defined against competitors in the customer's mind, not in absolute product terms.

## Reproducibility note
STP sequence and the 5 segmentation-effectiveness criteria reproducible from any recent edition of Kotler & Keller, *Marketing Management*. The "essence of strategic marketing" phrasing is widely attributed to Kotler and corroborated across independent framework summaries.
