## Key claims with exact numbers + locators (Methodology, Aug 2024)

1. **Four-tier hierarchy with exact 2024 counts:**
   - **11 Sectors**
   - **25 Industry Groups**
   - **74 Industries**
   - **163 Sub-Industries**
   (Corroborated across MSCI methodology, MSCI overview, and Fidelity/Wikipedia secondary.)

2. **8-digit coding.** Each level is identified within an 8-digit code: 2 digits sector, 4 digits
   industry group, 6 digits industry, 8 digits sub-industry.

3. **Classification principle - principal business activity, revenue-primary.** Exact sense:
   companies are "classified by principal business activity," with **revenue** the key
   determinant; **earnings** and **market perception** are "also recognized as important and
   relevant information for classification purposes, and are taken into account during the annual
   review process." A company receives **one** GICS classification at each of the four tiers.

4. **Annual review + ongoing maintenance.** GICS structure is reviewed annually; company
   classifications are reviewed and updated on a defined maintenance cycle.

5. **Contrast with NAICS.** GICS groups by what a company *does in the market* (its principal
   revenue-generating business / how investors perceive it), i.e. a more **demand-/market-oriented**
   lens, whereas NAICS groups by **production process** (supply-oriented). This is the key reason to
   hold BOTH as parameters (see BEST-PARTS).
