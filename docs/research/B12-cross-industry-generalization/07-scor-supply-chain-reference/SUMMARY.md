# SUMMARY — SCOR: Supply Chain Operations Reference model (ASCM)

## Full citation
- **Title:** *Supply Chain Operations Reference (SCOR) Model* / *SCOR Digital Standard (SCOR DS)*
- **Authors/Org:** Originally Supply-Chain Council (developed 1996 by PRTM [now PwC] and AMR
  Research [now Gartner]); now maintained by ASCM (Association for Supply Chain Management).
- **Year:** 1996 (introduced); SCOR DS current digital standard (2020+).
- **Venue/Publisher:** ASCM (professional standards body).
- **URLs:**
  - https://www.ascm.org/corporate-solutions/standards-tools/scor-ds/
  - https://www.ascm.org/globalassets/ascm_website_assets/docs/intro-and-front-matter-scor-digital-standard.pdf
  - https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supply_chain_operations_reference (pointer to primary)

## Ontology questions informed
- **L1.B12.1** — a second, independent, decades-proven example of a CROSS-INDUSTRY configurable
  process reference model, corroborating the invariant-spine + per-instance-configuration thesis in
  a different domain (operations) than APQC/C-EPC.

## GRADE tier
**Moderate** (authoritative professional standard, not peer-reviewed). Up-rated by ~30 years of
cross-industry adoption as "the cross-industry de facto standard" diagnostic tool for supply chains.

## Key claims with exact wording + locators

1. **Explicitly cross-industry.** Exact quote (Wikipedia summary of the primary):
   > "...endorsed by the Supply Chain Council, now part of ASCM, as the cross-industry de facto
   > standard strategy, performance management, and process improvement diagnostic tool for supply
   > chain management."

2. **A small, universal top-level process spine.** SCOR describes supply-chain activity as a fixed
   set of top-level processes: **Plan, Source, Make, Deliver, Return, Enable** (the SCOR DS uses
   Orchestrate/Plan/Order/Source/Transform/Fulfill/Return in the newest digital revision). This
   handful of processes is the **invariant spine**; specific industries configure the levels beneath.

3. **Four model components.** SCOR consists of: (a) standard process definitions; (b) performance
   **metrics / benchmarks** attached to those processes; (c) **best practices**; and (d) selection /
   configuration of the specific supply chain (skills/people in SCOR DS). I.e. process spine +
   metrics + best practice + configuration - the same anatomy as APQC PCF + C-EPC combined.

4. **Hierarchical + configurable to the specific chain.** SCOR is decomposed into levels; the top
   levels are universal process types, and lower levels are configured "into the configured specific
   supply chain" for a given company - explicit build-once/configure-per-instance design.

5. **SCOR DS evolution.** SCOR DS is "open-access and fully digital... even more comprehensive...
   with the inclusion of sustainability standards and supply-chain orchestration enablers."

## Reproducibility note
The Plan/Source/Make/Deliver/Return/Enable spine and the four-component anatomy are stated in the
ASCM SCOR DS front-matter PDF and the CIO/ISM practitioner descriptions; the "cross-industry de
facto standard" phrasing is the canonical Supply-Chain-Council/ASCM characterization.
