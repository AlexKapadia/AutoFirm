## Key claims with exact wording + locators

1. **Production-oriented grouping (Census).** Exact quote:
   > "NAICS is based on a production-oriented concept, meaning that it groups establishments into
   > industries according to similarity in the processes used to produce goods or services."

2. **Five levels of detail, 2-6 digit codes (Census).** The system is a "two-through-six-digit
   hierarchical classification" with five levels:
   - 1st-2nd digit -> **Industry Sector**
   - 3rd digit -> **Industry Subsector**
   - 4th digit -> **Industry Group**
   - 5th digit -> **Industry** (NAICS Industry; internationally comparable)
   - 6th digit -> country-specific **National Industry**
   > "The sixth digit in the NAICS code was designated as 'country specific' and represents
   > important economic industries particular to the United States."

3. **Supply-based rationale (StatCan).** StatCan states the production-oriented concept "best
   responds to the need for the three countries to have a framework to collect and publish
   information on inputs and outputs together and consistently."

4. **20 sectors -> subsectors -> industry groups -> industries (StatCan/Census).**
   > "NAICS classifies all economic activities into 20 sectors. These are subdivided into
   > subsectors, industry groups, and industries."
