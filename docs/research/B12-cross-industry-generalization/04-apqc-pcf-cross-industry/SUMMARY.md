## GRADE tier
**Moderate-to-High (Low as peer-review, but authoritative primary professional artifact).**
The PCF is the world's most widely used business-process framework (developed 1992 by APQC with
members across many industries/countries), used as the basis for Open Standards Benchmarking. It is
a *practitioner/standards* artifact, not peer-reviewed -> tiered **Moderate** per DEPTH-RUBRIC §2,
up-rated by its extremely broad, multi-industry, decades-long adoption and its explicit
cross-industry-vs-industry-specific design.

## Key claims with exact wording + locators (Intro to the PCF)

1. **A common cross-industry process language.** Exact quote:
   > "APQC's Process Classification Framework (PCF) creates a common language to discuss,
   > benchmark, and organize the work that businesses perform."
   And: it "allows organizations to objectively track and compare their performance internally and
   externally with organizations **from any industry**."

2. **Five hierarchical levels.** The PCF is "a hierarchical list of business processes" with five
   levels: **Category -> Process Group -> Process -> Activity -> Task.** Hierarchy numbers (e.g.
   "4.3.1") locate any element; each element has a unique, stable **process element identification
   number** that "allows for benchmarking even when process element names and definitions change
   across industries and organizations."

3. **13 Categories — the cross-industry invariants.** Exact quote:
   > "The PCF's 13 Categories include operating processes such as 2.0, 'Develop and Manage Products
   > and Services,' as well as management and support services such as 7.0, 'Develop and Manage
   > Human Capital.'"
   The 13 top-level categories split into **operating processes** (1.0-6.0: develop vision/strategy;
   develop/manage products & services; market & sell; deliver products & services; manage customer
   service) and **management & support services** (7.0-13.0: human capital; IT; financial
   resources; assets; enterprise risk/compliance/resiliency; external relationships; business
   capabilities).

4. **Cross-industry vs industry-specific by design.** Exact sense:
   > "It is designed as a framework and global standard to be customized for use in any
   > organization. In addition to the cross-industry PCF, APQC provides additional versions of the
   > PCF that include industry-specific processes and nuances."
   -> The HIGH levels (Category, Process Group, Process) are stable cross-industry; the LOW levels
   (Activity, Task) "often vary among industries and organizations."

5. **What the PCF is NOT.** It is "not a process map, a flow chart, or a swim lane diagram"; it is a
   taxonomy/list, not a control-flow model.
