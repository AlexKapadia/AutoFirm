## Key claims (with exact phrasing)
Proposes a **five-level autonomy-based taxonomy** for AI agents, framed by liability allocation:
- **Lower Levels (1–2):** "AI agents perform narrowly defined tasks with substantial user oversight; liability largely remains with the user."
- **Intermediate Levels (3–4):** "Responsibility begins transitioning towards developers and integrators who enable the agent's advanced decision-making capabilities."
- **Highest Level (5):** "AI agents independently decide and execute tasks with minimal human intervention; developers and providers bear greater liability."

**Three dimensions distinguishing levels:**
1. **Generality and scope of functions** — narrow/predefined vs. broad/open-ended.
2. **Control** — who decides when and how agents act (user vs. system).
3. **Access to external tools/environments** — closed systems vs. open web access.

**Core mechanism:** control and liability allocation shifts from users to developers as autonomy increases, mirroring the UK Automated Vehicles Act (which itself echoes SAE J3016's level structure).
