## Key claims (exact)

**The five coordinating mechanisms (Mintzberg 1979):**
1. **Mutual adjustment** — "coordination through informal communication and collaboration among
   peers"; control rests "in the hands of the doers."
2. **Direct supervision** — "coordination through a single leader giving orders and monitoring."
3. **Standardization of work processes** — "detailed procedures and methods define how work is
   done."
4. **Standardization of outputs** — "Outputs are standardized when the results of the work, for
   example the dimensions of a product [or the performance], are specified" — i.e. "targets and
   performance specifications define WHAT to deliver, leaving methods flexible."
5. **Standardization of skills** — "professional training and norms guide work; individuals
   coordinate based on shared expertise."
(Mintzberg later added a sixth, **standardization of norms.**)

**Mechanism <-> configuration mapping:**
- **Standardization of outputs -> Divisionalized Form** (semi-autonomous units, clear
  performance targets, flexible methods; fits multi-business / multi-geography).
- **Mutual adjustment -> Adhocracy** (innovation-intensive, dynamic environments; R&D, product
  development, digital ventures).
- (Work processes -> Machine Bureaucracy; skills -> Professional Bureaucracy; direct supervision
  -> Simple Structure.)

## Reproducibility note
The five mechanisms and their configuration mappings are reproduced identically across the
primary monograph and independent secondary analyses (diva2:251645; ACCA/Lunenburg 2012). The
"standardization of outputs = specify results, leave methods flexible" definition is verbatim
from the standard summaries of Mintzberg's text.
