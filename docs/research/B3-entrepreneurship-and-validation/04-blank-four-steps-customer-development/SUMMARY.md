## Core claims / the four steps
Central thesis: **a startup is not a smaller version of a large company**; its primary risk is
**building a product nobody wants**. Therefore: **discover customers first, then build.** The
**Customer Development** model has four steps:
1. **Customer Discovery** — turn the founder vision into hypotheses about customers/problems and test
   whether a real, important customer problem exists (problem/solution fit).
2. **Customer Validation** — build a repeatable, scalable sales/business model proven with early
   ("earlyvangelist"/visionary) customers; confirms a roadmap others will follow.
3. **Customer Creation** — scale demand generation to mainstream customers (crossing the chasm), only
   AFTER validation.
4. **Company Building** — transition from a learning-and-search organization to an
   execution-and-scale organization (departments, processes).

- **Key discipline — search vs. execution:** the first two steps *search for* a repeatable business
  model; only after validation does the company shift to *executing* it.
- **Iterate/pivot:** discovery and validation loop until problem-solution and business-model fit are
  proven; failing validation sends you back, not forward.

## Relationship to other sources
Direct intellectual parent of Lean Startup (source 03) and of the "validate before scale" logic the
RCTs test (sources 01/02). The search-vs-execution distinction maps onto AutoFirm's gate model
(search = validation phase; execution = build/operate phases).

## Reproducibility note
Definitional practitioner source; effectiveness corroborated by the independent RCT/SLR evidence in
this library. The four-step taxonomy is reproduced from Blank's own structure.
