## Key findings (exact)
- **Scale:** **759 firms across 4 randomized control trials.** Confirms a scientific approach to
  entrepreneurial decisions **can be taught** and **leads to superior results.**
- **Revenue (precise, significant):** treated firms earned **EUR 6,999.327 more** than control firms
  (**p = .030**). This is the precise estimate the 2020 paper could not pin down.
- **Idea termination:** positive impact on idea termination (treated firms more likely to terminate
  non-viable ideas) - replicating source 01.
- **Radical pivots (NONLINEAR):** results consistent with a **nonlinear effect on radical pivots** -
  treated firms tend to run **a few pivots rather than zero OR repeated/excessive pivots.** I.e.
  the scientific approach produces *disciplined* pivoting, not thrashing.

## Why the nonlinearity matters
The original suggested "more pivots = good"; the replication refines this: the optimum is a
**moderate** number of well-evidenced pivots. Too few = over-persistence; too many = thrashing
without convergence. The scientific approach pushes founders toward the productive middle.

## Reproducibility note
Direct, larger replication of the 2020 *Management Science* RCT with consistent direction of effect;
the EUR 6,999.327 (p=.030) revenue figure and the nonlinear-pivot finding are stated in the SMJ
abstract / results. Re-derivable from the open-access City University repository copy.
