## Core claims / definitions (exact)
- **ODI definition:** "Outcome-driven innovation is a strategy and innovation process developed by
  Anthony W. Ulwick. It is built around the theory that people buy products and services to get jobs
  done." It identifies and prioritizes customer outcomes that are "either important but poorly served
  or unimportant but over-served."
- **Desired outcome statements:** customers' needs are captured as measurable "desired outcomes"
  (e.g., "minimize the time it takes to ...").
- **Opportunity Algorithm (the formula, exact):**
  `Opportunity = Importance + max(Importance - Satisfaction, 0)`
  where **Importance** = the proportion (%) of a representative sample rating an outcome **very or
  extremely important**, and **Satisfaction** = the proportion (%) rating it **very or extremely
  satisfied**. (Some statements use the simpler `Importance + (Importance - Satisfaction)`; the
  `max(..., 0)` form floors the gap at zero.) Customers rate importance and satisfaction on a
  **1-to-10 scale**.
- **Interpretation:** the algorithm **double-weights importance** relative to the satisfaction gap,
  surfacing **important-but-unsatisfied** outcomes as the best growth opportunities — distinguishing
  it from a plain importance-minus-satisfaction gap analysis.
- **Claimed efficacy (vendor):** Strategyn's 2010 study reported **86% of projects** using the
  methodology were rated successful by sponsoring companies (vendor data — Low tier, cite with caveat).
