# BEST-PARTS — Cassens (2021) SLR + corroborating SLRs

## ADOPT
1. **Anchor the validation playbook on the two robust constructs: MVP + PIVOT.** The literature
   consistently reduces Lean Startup to these (Contigiani & Levinthal 2019). AutoFirm builds its
   L2.B3 engine on a small set of well-evidenced primitives (test/MVP, pivot, validated learning),
   not a sprawling unproven framework — keeps it simple (CLAUDE SS5.2) and defensible.
2. **Treat the digital-skew as a GENERALIZATION mandate.** The evidence is thin outside digital
   firms, so AutoFirm must PROVE its validation playbook on the full fixed industry panel
   (manufacturing, food service, healthcare, etc.), not just SaaS — turning a known literature gap
   into an explicit test requirement (CLAUDE SS3.9 generality, SS4.5 golden panel). Any playbook that
   only works for digital/SaaS is an instant FAIL.
3. **Cite Frederiksen & Brem (2017) for the effectuation linkage** — Lean Startup leans on
   effectuation logic (experimentation over long-range planning under uncertainty); this justifies
   AutoFirm's iterate-test-pivot loop as theory-grounded, not ad hoc.

## REJECT / DEFER
- **Reject any claim that Lean Startup is "proven effective" in general** — the SLRs say the evidence
  is thin and skewed. AutoFirm's strongest effectiveness claim rests on the Camuffo RCTs (01/02), not
  on Lean Startup's popularity. Avoid over-claiming in evidence/.
- **Defer effectuation-vs-causation theory depth** to a dedicated note if L2 needs it; cite here.

## Build implication
The SLR sets two design constraints for L2.B3: (1) keep the engine to evidence-backed primitives
(MVP, pivot, validated learning); (2) the generalization golden set MUST include non-digital,
regulated, and physical industries — proving AutoFirm beats the literature's digital-only limitation.
