## Core claims (the two schools)
- **Jobs as Progress** (Christensen / Moesta / Klement, "demand-side"): a person "hires" a product to
  **make progress** in their life/circumstance — high-level, narrative, emotion-and-context-rich. A
  person hires a product **to become better**.
- **Jobs as Activity** (Ulwick / ODI, source 06): a job is a **process/task** a customer executes,
  decomposable into **measurable desired outcomes** that can be scored and prioritized. We hire a
  product **to perform tasks**.
- **Consequence of the split:** "there are too many interpretations of JTBD" — the term is
  contested; practitioners must state WHICH interpretation they use. Progress-school is richer for
  *discovery/positioning*; Activity-school is operational for *measurement/prioritization*.
