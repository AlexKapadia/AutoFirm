## Core claims / definitions (as described)
- **Lean startups follow a hypothesis-driven approach:** translate the founder vision into a set of
  **falsifiable business-model hypotheses**, then test them with a series of **minimum viable
  products (MVPs)** before committing resources.
- **Build-Measure-Learn loop:** build the smallest test (MVP) -> measure real customer response ->
  learn (validate/invalidate the hypothesis) -> iterate or **pivot**.
- **Pivot:** a structured course-correction — change one element of the business model, keep others —
  when a hypothesis is invalidated.
- **Validated learning:** progress is measured by hypotheses confirmed/disconfirmed with real
  evidence, not by features shipped.
- **Cognitive-bias mitigation:** the hypothesis-driven discipline **mitigates founder cognitive
  biases** (confirmation bias, overconfidence/over-optimism) by forcing falsifiable predictions and
  real tests.
- **Boundary conditions:** lean methods are **best suited under HIGH uncertainty** about
  customer/market; less so when the problem is well-understood or MVPs are infeasible (deep-tech with
  long build cycles, high regulatory/safety stakes).
