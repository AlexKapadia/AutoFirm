# SUMMARY — IAS 7 Statement of Cash Flows (IFRS Accounting Standard)

## Full citation
- **Title:** IAS 7 *Statement of Cash Flows*
- **Issuing body / Author:** International Accounting Standards Board (IASB) under the IFRS Foundation (originally IAS 7 issued by the IASC, 1992; revised; amended Jan 2016 *Disclosure Initiative* and May 2023 *Supplier Finance Arrangements*).
- **Year:** current consolidated edition (amendments through 2023).
- **Venue / Publisher:** IFRS Foundation, official issued-standards list.
- **URL:** https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ias-7-statement-of-cash-flows/
- **Corroborating standard (US GAAP, independent body):** FASB ASC 230 *Statement of Cash Flows* (same three-category structure; FASB explicitly permits direct or indirect method). ICAEW technical guidance corroborates direct/indirect treatment (https://www.icaew.com/...ias7...).

## Key claims (exact phrasing, quoted)

1. **Objective:** IAS 7 "prescribes how to present information in a statement of cash flows about how an entity's cash and cash equivalents changed during a period."

2. **Definition of cash & cash equivalents:**
   - "Cash comprises cash on hand and demand deposits."
   - "Cash equivalents are short-term, highly liquid investments that are readily convertible to known amounts of cash and that are subject to an insignificant risk of changes in value."

3. **The three mandatory cash-flow classifications:**
   - **Operating activities:** "the principal revenue-producing activities of the entity and other activities that are not investing or financing activities."
   - **Investing activities:** "the acquisition and disposal of long-term assets and other investments not included in cash equivalents."
   - **Financing activities:** "activities that result in changes in the size and composition of the contributed equity and borrowings of the entity."

4. **Two methods for operating cash flows:**
   - **Direct method:** "major classes of gross cash receipts and gross cash payments are disclosed."
   - **Indirect method:** "profit or loss is adjusted for the effects of transactions of a non-cash nature, any deferrals or accruals of past or future operating cash receipts or payments."
   - IAS 7 *encourages* the direct method, but per ICAEW the indirect method is used by "the vast majority of US companies, and apparently a majority of entities using IFRS." (So AutoFirm must support the indirect method as the real-world default.)

5. **2016 amendment:** entities must disclose changes in liabilities arising from financing activities (the "financing liabilities reconciliation").
