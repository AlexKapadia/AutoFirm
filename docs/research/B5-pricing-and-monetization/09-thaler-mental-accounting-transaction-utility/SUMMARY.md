## Key claims and EXACT formulations (faithful)
1. **Prospect theory value function (exact properties):** outcomes are evaluated as **gains/losses
   relative to a reference point** (not absolute wealth); the value function v(.) is **concave for
   gains, convex for losses, and steeper for losses** (loss aversion). Kahneman & Tversky estimate
   the loss-aversion coefficient lambda approx **2** -- "the pain of a loss outweighs the pleasure of
   an equivalent gain by a factor of roughly two to one."
2. **Mental accounting / two utilities (Thaler 1985, exact):** a purchase has **acquisition utility**
   (value of the good vs. money paid) and **transaction utility** = (reference/expected price -
   actual price). "If the price ... is equal to the mental reference price ..., the transaction value
   is zero. If the price is lower than the reference price, the transaction utility is positive."
   Total purchase utility = acquisition + transaction utility.
3. **Reference dependence:** WTP and perceived fairness are anchored to a **reference price**;
   raising price above the reference is coded as a *loss* (amplified ~2x by loss aversion),
   explaining price-increase resistance and the value of price-decrease framing.
4. **Framing (1981):** logically equivalent gain- vs loss-framings produce different choices ("95%
   survival" vs "5% mortality"); applies directly to how prices, fees, surcharges, and discounts are
   framed (surcharge vs. discount asymmetry).
