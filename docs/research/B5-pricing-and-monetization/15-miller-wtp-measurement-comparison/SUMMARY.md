# SUMMARY — How should WTP be measured? Empirical comparison (Miller, Hofstetter, Krohmer, Zhang 2011)

> **Why this folder exists (AMBER fix).** The WTP-measurement space (sources 05 PSM, 06
> Gabor-Granger/conjoint, 07 elasticity) under-surveyed one real alternative: **incentive-compatible
> / experimental WTP elicitation** and the **hypothetical-bias** problem that afflicts stated-WTP
> methods. DEPTH-RUBRIC §4 requires the *full* method space with adopt/reject rationale. This
> peer-reviewed JMR paper benchmarks the leading WTP methods against REAL purchases and is the
> primary evidence on which to trust / down-weight.

## Key claims and numbers (faithful)
1. **The four methods compared (exact).** The study compares four WTP-measurement approaches against
   **REAL** purchase data as the benchmark: (a) **open-ended (OE)** direct question; (b)
   **choice-based conjoint (CBC)**; (c) the **Becker-DeGroot-Marschak (BDM)** incentive-compatible
   mechanism; and (d) **incentive-aligned choice-based conjoint (ICBC)**. (Source: abstract + study.)
2. **Headline finding (exact).** "The results indicate that the **BDM and ICBC approaches can pass
   statistical and decision-oriented tests**" against the REAL benchmark, whereas the
   non-incentive-aligned methods (OE, CBC) tend to **overstate** WTP. Incentive alignment is the key
   variable that brings stated WTP close to real WTP.
3. **Hypothetical bias (faithful).** Purely **hypothetical / stated-preference** methods (OE,
   standard CBC) suffer **hypothetical bias** - respondents report WTP higher than they actually pay
   because the question carries no real consequence. **Incentive-compatible** designs (BDM, ICBC),
   where the answer can result in a real purchase, mitigate this bias.
4. **BDM mechanism (Becker-DeGroot-Marschak 1964, faithful).** The respondent states a buying price;
   a price is then drawn at random; if the drawn price <= the stated price the respondent must buy at
   the **drawn** price, else not. Because the respondent cannot influence the price they pay by
   misstating, **truthful WTP reporting is the dominant strategy** - i.e. the mechanism is
   incentive-compatible. (This is why it tracks REAL purchases in Miller et al.)
5. **Decision-relevance over raw fit.** The paper judges methods not only on statistical closeness to
   REAL but on **decision-relevant metrics** (would a manager set the same price?) - so a method can
   be "good enough" for pricing decisions even if imperfect point-by-point.
