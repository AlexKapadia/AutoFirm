## Key claims (faithful)
1. **Monetization-model menu (the digital alternative space):**
   - **Flat-rate / subscription:** single recurring price; simple, predictable.
   - **Tiered ("Good-Better-Best", GBB):** multiple packages of increasing value; buyers self-select
     (= second-degree price discrimination, source 03).
   - **Per-seat / per-user:** "the most common model for B2B SaaS ... predictable for sellers,
     intuitive for buyers, scales with team growth." Breaks when value is not seat-correlated.
   - **Usage-based (consumption):** charge per unit consumed (API calls, compute, data); "aligns
     ... where usage varies significantly ... removes barriers to entry but lets you capture upside
     as usage grows." (= metered/nonlinear pricing.)
   - **Feature-based / per-feature:** charge by feature access (a form of versioning).
   - **Hybrid (subscription + usage):** "a recurring base charge ... plus consumption-based charges
     that scale with ... use" (= two-part tariff, source 03).
   - **Outcome-based:** charge per business result delivered (emerging, esp. AI).
2. **Freemium (exact):** "Freemium is not a standalone pricing model but an acquisition strategy ...
   layered on top of other ... structures." Free tier acquires; conversion to paid monetizes.
   Niculescu & Wu (2014) show free-version optimality depends on network effects & word-of-mouth.
3. **2025 trend (exact):** "outcome-based and usage-based plans now growing at the same pace as
   subscriptions, which previously dominated" (Monetization Monitor 2025) -- shift toward
   consumption/outcome alignment, especially for AI products.
4. **Pricing metric = the value metric.** The chosen *unit* (seat, API call, outcome) should track
   the value the customer receives -- the central design decision in digital monetization.
