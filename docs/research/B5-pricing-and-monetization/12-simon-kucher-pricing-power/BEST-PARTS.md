# BEST PARTS — Pricing Power & Profit Leverage

## ADOPT
- **The price-profit-leverage calculation as a per-client, deterministic computation.** Do NOT reuse
  the ~8% headline; instead build `profit_leverage(price_delta_pct, P&L) -> operating_profit_delta`
  from the client's REAL income statement (L1.B4.1 three-statement model; L1.B4.4 public data). This
  turns the "price is the strongest lever" thesis into an exact, client-specific number for the
  evidence/business case (CLAUDE sec 3.10/3.11). Boundary-tested (high vs low contribution margin
  changes leverage dramatically).
- **Value-based-over-cost-plus as the playbook's default stance, now evidence-backed.** Sources 01,
  02, and 12 jointly justify making value-based pricing the target and cost-plus the floor (closes
  the loop opened in source 01). Build implication: the strategy selector defaults to value-based
  when an EVC model can be built; cost-plus only as floor/fallback.
- **The realization gap as a built-in caution + workflow.** Because firms capture only ~half of
  attempted increases, AutoFirm pairs every increase recommendation with (a) staged increments and
  (b) value communication (sources 02/09) to improve realization. Build implication: price-increase
  recommendations include a realization-risk estimate and a communication plan.
- **"Pricing power" as a diagnostic AutoFirm scores per client** (differentiation, switching costs,
  competitive intensity) to decide how aggressively to price toward EVC.

## REJECT / DEFER
- **Reject quoting the ~8% (or any single survey number) as a client's expected result** -- it is
  company-structure-dependent (overfitting trap, CLAUDE sec 3.9). Always compute from the client's
  own P&L.
- **Defer** Simon-Kucher's industry benchmark tables -> useful context, not relied-upon numbers
  (vendor Low tier).
