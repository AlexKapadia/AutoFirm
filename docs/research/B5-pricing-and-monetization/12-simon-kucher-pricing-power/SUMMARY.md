## Key claims and numbers (faithful)
1. **Profit leverage of price (Marn & Rosiello, exact mechanism):** price is the most powerful
   profit lever; a **1% improvement in price**, holding volume, yields a far larger operating-profit
   improvement than a 1% improvement in volume, variable cost, or fixed cost -- in the HBR analysis
   of a sample of S&P companies, a **1% price rise produced on average an ~8-11% increase in
   operating profit** (the exact figure varies by company cost structure; ~8% is the most-cited
   average from the 1992 study). [Important claim; magnitude is company-specific.]
2. **Cost-plus underperforms (Simon-Kucher):** "By aligning prices with the unique value delivered
   ..., businesses can achieve greater profit impact than through equivalent changes in volume or
   costs." Value-based pricing beats cost-plus on profit.
3. **Realization gap (exact):** firms "manage to get only half of what they ask for" on price
   increases; "over 80% of companies have come under increased pricing pressure, and almost 60% ...
   are embroiled in a price war" (Global Pricing Study). Pricing remains an *underused* lever.
4. **Pricing power (definition):** "the ability of a company to get the prices it deserves for the
   value it delivers to customers" -- built via value quantification + communication (sources 01/02).

## Reproducibility note

firm's P&L (operating-leverage arithmetic). The realization gap / pricing-pressure stats are from the

and recorded as such (down-rate on precision per DEPTH-RUBRIC sec 2) -- AutoFirm computes the exact
