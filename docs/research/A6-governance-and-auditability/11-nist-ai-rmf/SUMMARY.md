## GRADE tier
**High.** Official US government framework (NIST), widely adopted as the reference for AI governance. Authoritative primary source for the governance function model; voluntary (not binding) but institution-standard.

## Core — four functions
The AI RMF Core comprises four functions, each broken into categories/subcategories:
- **GOVERN** (cross-cutting, central): "cultivates a culture of AI risk management, establishes accountability, defines policies and processes, and ensures oversight across the AI lifecycle."
- **MAP:** establish context, categorize the system, clarify capabilities/goals, map risks (incl. third-party), characterize impacts.
- **MEASURE:** "quantitative and qualitative tools, techniques, and methodologies to analyze, assess, benchmark, and monitor AI risk."
- **MANAGE:** prioritize and respond to risks found via MAP/MEASURE — mitigation, incident response, etc.

## The closed loop
MAP (identify) → MEASURE (assess/monitor) → MANAGE (respond), all under GOVERN (accountability + oversight). This is the governance analogue of A6.3's closed-loop enforcement: measurement must feed management action, with documented accountability — not measurement for its own sake.

## Documentation/accountability
GOVERN emphasizes accountability structures and documented policies; the accompanying Playbook provides suggested actions and is a living, periodically-updated resource.
