## Core model (exact definitions, quoted)
PROV-DM models provenance with three core **types** and a set of **relations** between them.

Three types (verbatim from the Recommendation):
- **Entity:** "A physical, digital, conceptual, or other kind of thing with some fixed aspects; entities may be real or imaginary."
- **Activity:** "Something that occurs over a period of time and acts upon or with entities; it may include consuming, processing, transforming, modifying, relocating, using, or generating entities."
- **Agent:** "Something that bears some form of responsibility for an activity taking place, for the existence of an entity, or for another agent's activity."

Seven core relations (verbatim definitions):
1. **Used** — "The beginning of utilizing an entity by an activity."
2. **WasGeneratedBy** — "The completion of production of a new entity by an activity. This entity did not exist before generation."
3. **WasInformedBy** — "The exchange of some unspecified entity by two activities, one activity using some entity generated by the other."
4. **WasDerivedFrom** — "A transformation of an entity into another, an update of an entity resulting in a new one, or construction based on pre-existing entity."
5. **WasAttributedTo** — "The ascribing of an entity to an agent."
6. **WasAssociatedWith** — "An assignment of responsibility to an agent for an activity, indicating that the agent had a role in the activity."
7. **ActedOnBehalfOf** — "The assignment of authority and responsibility to an agent to carry out a specific activity as delegate or representative."

## Six components (organisation of PROV-DM)
1. Entities and Activities (generation, usage, start, end, invalidation, communication).
2. Derivations (and subtypes: revision, quotation, primary source).
3. Agents, Responsibility, and Influence.
4. **Bundles** — "a named set of provenance descriptions, and is itself an entity, so allowing provenance of provenance to be expressed."
5. Alternate (relations linking entities referring to the same thing).
6. Collections (membership relations).

## Why it matters (the "what/when/who" mapping)
PROV-DM's three types answer the canonical audit questions directly: **what** = Entity, **how/when** = Activity (with start/end times), **who** = Agent (`wasAssociatedWith` for the actor; `actedOnBehalfOf` for delegation). The `actedOnBehalfOf` delegation relation and `wasInformedBy` activity-to-activity link map onto multi-agent orchestration, where one agent acts as a delegate of another and activities hand off entities.
