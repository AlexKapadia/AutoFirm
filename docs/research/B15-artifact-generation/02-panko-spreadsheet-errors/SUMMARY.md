## GRADE tier
**High** for the error-taxonomy and confidence-gap findings (peer-reviewed journal + repeated independent corroboration over 15+ years, the canonical reference in the field); **Moderate** for any single exact percentage (lab/field studies vary). Up-rated: large, consistent effect replicated across many independent studies.

## Key claims (faithful, exact numbers + context)
1. **Cell Error Rate (CER):** across studies the per-cell error rate falls in the **low single-digit percent** range (commonly cited ~**1–5%**, with summary figures around **3%** of cells containing errors in audited models). Quoted comparison: error rates of **0.5% for "simple mechanical tasks"** and **~5% for "complex logical activities" like writing computer programs** — spreadsheet CERs sit in this human-error band.
2. **Spreadsheet-level error prevalence:** the probability that a non-trivial spreadsheet contains at least one error is in the **~80–90%** range; many field audits found errors in nearly every spreadsheet reviewed (~90%).
3. **Error taxonomy:** **mechanical** errors (mistyping a number, pointing to the wrong cell), **logic** errors (wrong formula from a reasoning mistake), and the most dangerous **omission** errors (something left out — hardest to detect).
4. **Confidence gap (over-confidence):** when asked to estimate the probability their spreadsheet contained an error, individuals had a **mean estimate of 18%** (triads 13%), while actual proportion of incorrect spreadsheets was **86% for individuals and 27% for triads** — humans massively under-estimate their own error rate; review by a group reduces but does not eliminate errors.

## Reproducibility note
Figures are reproduced from Panko's canonical summary page and the EuSpRIG paper; the 80–90% prevalence and the 0.5%/5% task-error comparison are the most-cited and independently corroborated across the EuSpRIG corpus. Single exact percentages should be quoted with "Panko reports ~X" given inter-study variance.
