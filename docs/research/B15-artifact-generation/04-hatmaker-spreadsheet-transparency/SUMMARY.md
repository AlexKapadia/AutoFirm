## Key claims (faithful)
1. **Problem:** "Auditors demand financial models be transparent yet no consensus exists on what that means precisely." There is no objective way to evaluate transparency, so debate over which methods are "more or less transparent" stays subjective.
2. **Proposal:** a **concrete definition of spreadsheet transparency** specific enough to enable (a) measurement tools and (b) automation, so auditors can verify compliance with transparency requirements programmatically.
3. **Measures:** quantifiable transparency measures that let a modeller objectively assess different spreadsheet construction methods and pick the one that best meets their transparency objectives — moving from subjective debate to an evidence-based, *measurable* framework.
4. Implication (faithful to the proposal's intent): transparency is a property of how formulas reference and expose their logic — short, self-explaining, consistently-laid-out formulas are measurably more transparent than long, opaque, constant-laden ones (the same direction as FAST).
