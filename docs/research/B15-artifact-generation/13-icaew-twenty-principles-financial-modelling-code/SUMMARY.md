## GRADE tier
**High** as a primary, named, governance-body standard authored by a chartered-accountancy
institute and synthesized from multiple modelling methodologies. Up-rated: it is an *independent*
corroboration of FAST/Operis/SSRB convergence (the Financial Modelling Code "took a half-dozen
methodologies and pared down to the commonly-held, widely-accepted beliefs"), and ICAEW *officially
recognizes* the FAST Standard — two structurally-independent bodies endorse the same core rules.

## Key claims (faithful, exact wording where load-bearing)
1. **The Twenty Principles** (exact headings, grouped as published):
   *Business environment:* 1 determine role & plan standards; 2 adopt/maintain organizational
   standards; 3 ensure knowledge/competence; 4 work collaboratively with peer review.
   *Designing & building:* 5 confirm spreadsheet is the right tool; 6 identify the audience;
   7 include a documentation sheet; 8 design for longevity; 9 focus on required outputs;
   **10 "Separate and clearly identify inputs, workings and outputs"**; **11 be consistent in
   structure**; **12 be consistent in the use of formulae**; **13 keep formulae short and simple**;
   **14 "Never embed in a formula anything that might change or need to be changed"**;
   **15 "Perform a calculation once and then refer back to that calculation"**; 16 avoid advanced
   features where simpler ones suffice.
   *Risks & controls:* 17 backup & version control; 18/19 build in checks/controls/alerts and test
   rigorously; 20 protect parts not meant to be changed.
2. **Convergence with FAST (resolves the prior single-source gap):**
   ICAEW P10 (separate inputs/workings/outputs), P12 (consistent formulae), P13 (short/simple),
   P14 (no embedded constants), P15 (calculate-once-then-reference) are the *same* structural rules
   FAST encodes — derived independently by a different standards body. ICAEW also formally
   recognizes FAST ("the first standard for financial modeling to be officially recognized by the
   ICAEW").
3. **Motivation (independent of Panko):** the Twenty Principles exist to reduce wasted time and
   "(sometimes costly) errors"; ICAEW cites that "approximately 90% of spreadsheets contain
   mistakes" — an independent restatement of the Panko/Powell prevalence finding.
4. **Financial Modelling Code:** synthesizes ~half-a-dozen modelling methodologies into commonly
   held best practice; read with the Twenty Principles (cross-referenced); family also includes the
   *Spreadsheet Competency Framework* and a *Financial Modelling Best Practice Guideline*.
