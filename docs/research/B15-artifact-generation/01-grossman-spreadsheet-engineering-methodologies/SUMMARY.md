## Key claims (faithful)
1. Large financial planning models are "written in a spreadsheet programming language (usually Microsoft Excel) and deployed as a spreadsheet application" — a spreadsheet is software and should be engineered as such.
2. The paper identifies **three independent standardized methodologies**, each giving "detailed guidance on design, construction process, and quality control":
   - **FAST Alliance** (FAST Standard — Flexible, Appropriate, Structured, Transparent).
   - **Operis Group** (heavy use of named ranges; highly systematic build process).
   - **Spreadsheet Standards Review Board (SSRB)** — developed by BPM Analytics.
3. Independently developed yet convergent goals: they provide "enhanced productivity, accuracy and maintainability for large financial planning models."
4. Shared convergent discipline: rigorous **consistent layout**; **separation of inputs / calculations / outputs**; **one formula consistently across a row** (copy-coherence); avoiding embedded constants; documentation; structured QC/review.
