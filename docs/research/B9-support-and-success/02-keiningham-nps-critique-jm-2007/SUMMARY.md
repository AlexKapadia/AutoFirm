## Ontology question informed
- **L1.B9.1** — peer-reviewed critique gating the NPS predictive claim.

## What the source claims (faithful)
- Uses **longitudinal data from 21 firms and 15,500+ interviews** from the **Norwegian Customer Satisfaction Barometer** to replicate the Net Promoter analyses and compare against the American Customer Satisfaction Index (ACSI).
- **Finding:** the study **failed to replicate** Reichheld's assertions regarding the "clear superiority" of Net Promoter as a predictor of firm revenue growth versus other customer-loyalty/satisfaction measures, even in the industries Reichheld cited as exemplars.
- Conclusion: there is **no empirical evidence** that the "likelihood to recommend" question is a *better* predictor of business growth than other customer-loyalty questions (e.g., satisfaction, repurchase intent).
