## What the source claims (faithful)
- Distinguishes **Customer Success (CS)** — proactive, outcome-driven management of the post-sale customer relationship to drive retention and expansion — from reactive **Customer Support**.
- **Core economic premise:** in recurring-revenue (SaaS/subscription) businesses, survival depends on **reducing/managing churn** and **growing Net Revenue Retention (NRR)** — expanding contract value from existing customers.
- **Customer Health Score:** a predictive composite metric combining **product-usage signals, support patterns, relationship quality, and financial signals** to forecast renewal/churn. Most implementations integrate **4–6 data sources** (product analytics, CRM, billing, support tickets).
  - **Key behavioural insight:** **rate of change predicts churn better than absolute level** — a customer declining from 85→65 is higher risk than one stable at 60.
- The book codifies CS as a distinct organisational function (the rise of the Chief Customer Officer / CS team).

## Reproducibility note
The CS-vs-support distinction, the multi-signal health-score composition, the rate-of-change insight, and the churn/NRR framing are reproducible from the book and consistent across the SaaS CS literature. The churn-reduction percentage claims are excluded (vendor, Very-low). NRR definition is standard: NRR = (starting ARR + expansion − contraction − churn) / starting ARR.
