## Ontology question informed
- **L1.B9.1** — introduces the **Customer Effort Score (CES)** and the "effortless service" thesis.

## What the source claims (faithful)
- Based on a CEB study of **more than 75,000 people** who interacted with contact-centre representatives or used self-service channels.
- **Central finding:** "over-the-top" service efforts make little difference to loyalty — **"All customers really want is a simple, quick solution to their problem."** Reducing customer **effort** is a stronger driver of loyalty than exceeding expectations ("delight").
- Introduces the **Customer Effort Score (CES):** a single-item metric asking how much effort the customer had to expend to get their issue resolved. The authors report CES is a **better predictor of loyalty (repurchase + increased spend) than CSAT or NPS** in service-interaction contexts.
- **Five loyalty-building tactics** for service orgs:
  1. Reduce repeat calls by anticipating and dealing with related **downstream** issues (next-issue avoidance).
  2. Arm reps to address the **emotional** side of interactions.
  3. **Minimise channel switching** (don't force customers to switch channels).
  4. Elicit and use feedback from disgruntled/struggling customers.
  5. Focus on **problem solving, not speed** (don't over-optimise AHT at the cost of resolution).

## Source-quality grade (GRADE-adapted)
- **Tier: Low–Moderate.** Practitioner article (HBR) backed by a large proprietary CEB dataset (N>75,000). Large sample up-rates it; **proprietary, non-peer-reviewed methodology** and CEB's commercial interest in CES down-rate it.
- **Corroborated** by the same authors' later book (source 06, N>97,000) and consistent with Anderson & Sullivan (source 04, peer-reviewed disconfirmation theory) — convergent evidence raises confidence in the *direction* (effort matters) even if exact effect sizes are CEB-internal.

## Reproducibility note
Sample (75,000+), the "effort > delight" direction, and the five tactics are stated in the HBR article and reproduced in the open mirror PDF. CES's exact predictive-superiority numbers are CEB-internal and treated as Low-confidence; only the qualitative direction is relied upon.
