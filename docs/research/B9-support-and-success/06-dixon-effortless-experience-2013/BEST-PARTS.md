# BEST-PARTS — Dixon, Toman & DeLisi (2013) Effortless Experience

## ADOPT
- **An explicit "effort-event" taxonomy** AutoFirm's support agents are penalised for triggering: repeat contacts, channel switches, transfers, asking the customer to repeat info, robotic/scripted responses, policy friction.
  - **Build implication (L2.B9):** instrument each ticket with effort-event counters (n_transfers, n_channel_switches, n_repeats_of_info, n_recontacts_7d). These are deterministic, auditable signals feeding both CES and the health score (source 10). Test: an interaction that transfers + re-asks info must score worse than one resolved in-channel first-contact.
- **Self-service / deflection is justified by the 57% "tried web first" finding** — invest in knowledge-base + AI deflection *before* the customer reaches an agent, and ensure the website answer is findable (else they channel-switch to a call). Ties to source 09 deflection metrics.
- **"Manage expectations, then meet them"** + **FCR as the north-star quality metric** (over delight or speed).

## REJECT
- **REJECT** designing for "delight"/exceeding-expectations as the loyalty lever (no significant loyalty gain per N>97,000).
- **REJECT** point-estimate reuse: do NOT hard-code "94% repurchase" or "deflect 80%" as universal constants in AutoFirm outputs — these are context-specific CEB figures; use them as *directional priors*, parameterised per client/industry (CLAUDE §3.9 anti-overfit).

## Why (cited)
- Large-sample corroboration of source 05 and convergent with peer-reviewed source 04 → meets the ≥2-source bar for the effort-reduction design and the deflection investment rationale. The effort-event taxonomy is fully industry-general.
