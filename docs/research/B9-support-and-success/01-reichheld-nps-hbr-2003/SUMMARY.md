## What the source claims (faithful)
- Central thesis: a single survey question — **"How likely is it that you would recommend [company X] to a friend or colleague?"** — measured on a **0–10 scale**, predicts customer loyalty and company growth as well as or better than longer satisfaction surveys.
- **Categorisation of responses** (confirmed via the Wikipedia pointer, independently corroborated by Keiningham 2007 below):
  - **Promoters** = scores **9–10**
  - **Passives** = scores **7–8**
  - **Detractors** = scores **0–6**
- **Formula (reproduced exactly):**
  `Net Promoter Score = % Promoters − % Detractors`
  Passives are in the base (denominator) but neither added nor subtracted. Score ranges from **−100 to +100**.
- The article reports that, in the industries studied, the "would recommend" question correlated most strongly with repeat purchases and referrals, and therefore with organic growth.
