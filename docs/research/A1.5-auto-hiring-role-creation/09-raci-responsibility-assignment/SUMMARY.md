# Responsibility Assignment Matrix (RACI) and its variants

**Question informed:** L1.A1.5 (manager-authored role-spec / accountability); feeds L2.ORG, L2.A6.

## Full citation
- **Title:** Responsibility assignment matrix (RACI / RASCI / DACI / RAPID)
- **Author/Org:** PMI body-of-knowledge lineage; documented in the Project Management Institute,
  *A Guide to the Project Management Body of Knowledge (PMBOK Guide)* (RAM/RACI). Encyclopaedic
  synthesis: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Responsibility_assignment_matrix
- **DACI primary:** Intuit (popularised); **RAPID primary:** Bain & Company,
  "RAPID: Bain's tool to clarify decision accountability."
- **Year:** RACI is decades-established practitioner standard; PMBOK editions ongoing.
- **GRADE tier:** **Low-to-Moderate** (practitioner standard / professional body-of-knowledge, not
  empirical study). Treated as a *design pattern* source, not a quantitative claim; corroborated by
  role theory (source 04) for the underlying "one accountable owner reduces ambiguity" principle.

## What it is
A **role-clarity matrix** mapping each task/decision to people/roles by responsibility type, used
to eliminate ownership ambiguity and overlapping duties.

## Key claims (exact)
**RACI roles:**
- **R - Responsible:** does the work (executes the task). May be multiple.
- **A - Accountable:** owns the outcome, approves/signs off. **Exactly one per item** (the core
  rule - a single accountable owner).
- **C - Consulted:** two-way input is sought before/during.
- **I - Informed:** one-way updates after the fact.

**Core principle (exact):** "One person (role) is accountable per item; the Responsible does the
work while the Accountable owns the outcome and signs off." Vague ownership / unclear approvals /
wrong-time involvement are the named causes of slow delivery that RACI removes.

**Variants (same need, different emphasis):**
- **RASCI** - adds **S - Support**.
- **DACI** - **Driver, Approver, Contributors, Informed** (decision-making emphasis).
- **RAPID** (Bain) - **Recommend, Agree, Perform, Input, Decide** (decision-rights emphasis).
- **CAIRO/CARS** - adds **O - Omitted/Out-of-loop** to make exclusion explicit.

## Up/down-rate reasoning
Low-to-Moderate (practitioner pattern). Used only for the **structural** accountability design,
which is independently grounded by role theory (source 04: role ambiguity/conflict). The single-
accountable-owner rule is the relied-upon item and is corroborated by source 04.

## Reproducibility note
The four RACI letters, the single-accountable rule, and the DACI/RAPID/RASCI variants are
documented in the PMBOK Guide, Bain's RAPID note, and standard PM references; verifiable.
