# Toward a Theory of Organizational Socialization - the Six Socialization Tactics

**Question informed:** L1.A1.5 (onboard step); feeds L2.ORG.

## Full citation
- **Title:** Toward a Theory of Organizational Socialization
- **Authors:** John Van Maanen; Edgar H. Schein
- **Year:** 1979
- **Venue:** Research in Organizational Behavior, Vol. 1, pp. 209-264 (JAI Press)
- **URL/ref:** https://www.scirp.org/reference/referencespapers?referenceid=2695917
- **GRADE tier:** **High** - the foundational primary statement of socialization tactics; the
  six-tactic taxonomy is reproduced and meta-analysed across the field (corroborated by sources
  06 and 08).

## What it is
The original theory that organisations **structure newcomer experience** via six bipolar
**socialization tactics**, and that the choice of tactics shapes the newcomer's **role
orientation** (custodial/conforming vs. innovative/role-redefining).

## Key claims (exact)
**Definition.** Socialization tactics are "the ways in which the experiences of individuals in
transition from one role to another are structured for them by others in the organization."

**The six bipolar tactics (each a continuum):**
1. **Collective vs. individual** - newcomers socialised as a cohort vs. in isolation.
2. **Formal vs. informal** - segregated, prescribed programme vs. on-the-job, in situ.
3. **Sequential vs. random** - a known fixed sequence of steps vs. ambiguous/variable order.
4. **Fixed vs. variable** - a known timetable to role mastery vs. an unknown one.
5. **Serial vs. disjunctive** - experienced role models/mentors guide vs. no predecessor model.
6. **Investiture vs. divestiture** - newcomer's incoming identity/skills affirmed vs. stripped
   and rebuilt.

**Institutionalized vs. individualized pattern (the key grouping, later formalised by Jones 1986
and meta-analysed by Saks et al. 2007):** the "left" poles (collective, formal, sequential,
fixed, serial, investiture) cluster as **institutionalized** socialization and produce a
**custodial** orientation + lower role ambiguity/conflict + higher fit/commitment; the "right"
poles cluster as **individualized** and produce an **innovative/role-redefining** orientation.
Jones (1986) further grouped the six into **context** (collective+formal), **content**
(sequential+fixed), and **social** (serial+investiture) tactics.

## Up/down-rate reasoning
High tier: foundational primary theory, repeatedly operationalised (Jones 1986 instrument) and
meta-analysed (sources 06, 08). The six-tactic list is reproduced verbatim across the literature.

## Reproducibility note
The six bipolar tactics and the institutionalized/individualized grouping are reproduced
identically in Jones (1986, AMJ) and the Saks/Uggerslev/Fassina (2007) and Bauer et al.
(2007, 2025) meta-analyses; verifiable against any of them.
