# Newcomer Adjustment During Organizational Socialization: A Meta-Analytic Review

**Question informed:** L1.A1.5 (onboard step - what makes onboarding work); feeds L2.ORG.

## Full citation
- **Title:** Newcomer adjustment during organizational socialization: A meta-analytic review of
  antecedents, outcomes, and methods
- **Authors:** Talya N. Bauer; Todd Bodner; Berrin Erdogan; Donald M. Truxillo; Jennifer S. Tucker
- **Year:** 2007
- **Venue:** Journal of Applied Psychology, 92(3), 707-721
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0021-9010.92.3.707
- **Open copy:** https://pdxscholar.library.pdx.edu/busadmin_fac/27/
- **Empirical base:** meta-analysis + path modelling of **70 unique samples** of newcomers.
- **GRADE tier:** **High** - peer-reviewed meta-analysis in a top I/O journal; the canonical
  quantitative model of newcomer adjustment.

## What it is
A meta-analytic test of a mediation model: organizational **socialization tactics** and newcomer
**information seeking** (antecedents) drive newcomer **adjustment**, which in turn drives
distal **socialization outcomes**.

## Key claims (exact)
**Three indicators of newcomer adjustment (the mediators):**
1. **Role clarity** - understanding what the role requires.
2. **Self-efficacy** - confidence in performing the role.
3. **Social acceptance** - feeling liked/accepted by the work group.

**Distal outcomes mediated by adjustment:** job **performance**, job **satisfaction**,
organizational **commitment**, **intentions to remain**, and **turnover** (negative).

**Model structure (the load-bearing finding):** adjustment (role clarity, self-efficacy, social
acceptance) **mediates** the effects of socialization tactics and information seeking on the
outcomes - i.e. tactics work *through* adjustment, not directly. The meta-analytic path model
"generally supported" this mediation.

**Methodological moderators coded:** data-collection type (longitudinal vs. cross-sectional),
sample (school-to-work vs. work-to-work transition), and antecedent measurement (facet vs.
composite). These moderate the magnitudes but not the structure.

## Up/down-rate reasoning
High tier: peer-reviewed JAP meta-analysis, k=70 samples, path-modelled. The three-indicator
adjustment construct and the mediation structure are the relied-upon claims; both are central
findings of the paper and reproduced in Bauer & Erdogan's later reviews (sources 07, 08). Exact
per-path corrected correlations live in the paper's tables; the abstract-level structure (three
mediators, the five outcomes, mediation) is verified here and corroborated independently by
source 08 (the 2025 meta-analysis).

## Reproducibility note
The k=70-sample meta-analysis, the three adjustment indicators, and the mediation model are
stated in the abstract and Table/path-model of the JAP article and restated in Bauer & Erdogan
(2011) and Bauer et al. (2025). Verifiable via DOI 10.1037/0021-9010.92.3.707.
