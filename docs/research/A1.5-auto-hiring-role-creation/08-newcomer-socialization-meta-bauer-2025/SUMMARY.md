# New Horizons for Newcomer Organizational Socialization: A Review, Meta-Analysis, and Future Directions

**Question informed:** L1.A1.5 (onboard step - updated evidence); feeds L2.ORG.

## Full citation
- **Title:** New Horizons for Newcomer Organizational Socialization: A Review, Meta-Analysis, and
  Future Research Directions
- **Authors:** Talya N. Bauer; Berrin Erdogan; Allison M. Ellis; Donald M. Truxillo; Grant M.
  Brady; Todd Bodner
- **Year:** 2025
- **Venue:** Journal of Management (SAGE)
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1177/01492063241277168
- **URL:** https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/01492063241277168
- **GRADE tier:** **High** - peer-reviewed review + meta-analysis in a top management journal;
  the most current synthesis, updating the 2007 model (source 06).

## What it is
An updated review and meta-analysis of newcomer socialization that re-tests the adjustment model
and elevates **social acceptance/connection** as a central mechanism, and considers the
**evolving (remote/hybrid) future of work**.

## Key claims (exact)
- **Re-affirms the three adjustment indicators** (role clarity, self-efficacy, social acceptance)
  as the core mediators of socialization outcomes, consistent with Bauer et al. (2007, source 06).
- **Social acceptance is central.** Quoted (per the publisher abstract / Wiley companion piece,
  Bauer et al.): the meta-analysis "found **strong relationships between social acceptance and
  numerous outcomes** and positioned it as a **central feature of newcomer adjustment**." Earlier
  models over-weighted information/uncertainty reduction; the updated evidence centres **social
  connection** as the mechanism.
- **Newcomer agency / adaptability matters.** Modern socialization must capture **newcomer agency,
  agility, and the ability to manage ambiguity**; organisations should structure onboarding to
  facilitate adaptability - especially as work becomes remote/distributed.

## Up/down-rate reasoning
High tier: peer-reviewed meta-analytic review in Journal of Management (2025), by the same
research lineage as source 06, providing **independent corroboration** of the three-indicator
model and **updating** the relative weight on social acceptance. Together with source 06 this
gives the "onboarding adjustment model" >=2 independent High-tier meta-analytic sources.
