# Workforce Reshaping Operations Handbook - role retirement, redeployment, and RIF

**Question informed:** L1.A1.5 (the RETIRE step + redeploy-vs-eliminate); feeds L2.ORG.

## Full citation
- **Title:** Workforce Reshaping Operations Handbook
- **Author/Org:** U.S. Office of Personnel Management (OPM)
- **Year:** March 2017
- **Venue:** opm.gov (US federal HR authority - professional/primary government source)
- **URL:** https://www.opm.gov/policy-data-oversight/workforce-restructuring/reductions-in-force-rif/workforce_reshaping.pdf
- **Congressional summary (corroborating):** Congressional Research Service, IF12908,
  https://www.congress.gov/crs_external_products/IF/HTML/IF12908.html
- **GRADE tier:** **Moderate** (authoritative government operational handbook). Corroborated by the
  CRS summary (independent body) and by the broader restructuring literature for the
  redeploy-over-RIF principle.

## What it is
The US government's operational guidance for **workforce reshaping** - the disciplined process of
eliminating, consolidating, and reassigning positions when missions change, including
reorganisation, management-directed reassignment, transfer of function, and reduction in force
(RIF), with a strong preference for **avoiding involuntary elimination where possible**.

## Key claims (exact)
- **Drivers of role obsolescence:** a shift in mission/vision/products renders positions redundant;
  M&A or reorganisation creates **overlapping roles** that get consolidated; **technology/
  automation** decreases the need for specific roles. (Corroborated across the restructuring
  literature.)
- **Reshaping options menu:** reorganization, management-directed reassignment, furlough, transfer
  of function, and (last resort) reduction in force.
- **Redeploy / reassign before eliminate (exact):** "At its discretion, an agency may reassign an
  employee, **without regard to RIF procedures**, to a vacant position at the same grade and rate
  of pay" and then decline to staff the now-vacant position - i.e. absorb surplus by reassignment
  to **avoid a formal RIF**.
- **Time reduces the need for RIF (exact):** "the more time an organization has to implement its
  workforce reshaping, the less likely the need to conduct a formal reduction in force (RIF)" -
  i.e. **plan retirement of roles ahead, not reactively**.
- **Workforce planning identifies surplus first:** reshaping plans should use workforce planning
  and analysis to identify **surplus positions** due to reorganised missions, and mechanisms to
  avoid RIF where reshaping creates surplus.

## Up/down-rate reasoning
Moderate (government operational handbook). The relied-upon principle - **redeploy/reassign before
eliminate, plan retirement ahead** - is corroborated by the CRS summary and the general
restructuring literature (which also reports that redeployment-over-reduction retains institutional
knowledge and speeds recovery). Jurisdiction-specific RIF legal ordering is NOT relied upon.

## Reproducibility note
The reassign-without-RIF provision and the "more time -> less RIF" statement are quoted from the
OPM handbook and echoed in the CRS IF12908 summary; both public, retrievable.
