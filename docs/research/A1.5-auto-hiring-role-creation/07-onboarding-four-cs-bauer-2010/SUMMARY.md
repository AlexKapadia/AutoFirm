# Onboarding New Employees: Maximizing Success - the Four Cs

**Question informed:** L1.A1.5 (onboard step - operational onboarding content); feeds L2.ORG.

- **Author:** Talya N. Bauer
- **Year:** 2010
- **Venue:** SHRM Foundation (Society for Human Resource Management Foundation), Alexandria, VA
- **URL/ref:** https://www.scirp.org/reference/referencespapers?referenceid=3522838 ;

## What it is
The widely-adopted operational framework for **what onboarding must accomplish**, distilled into
the **Four Cs**, with an explicit ramp-up "levels" view of onboarding effectiveness.

## Key claims (exact)
**The Four Cs of onboarding (in increasing depth):**
1. **Compliance** - the lowest level: teaching basic legal/policy rules and providing access,
   credentials, and equipment.
2. **Clarification** - ensuring newcomers understand their **new jobs and all related
   expectations** (role clarity).
3. **Culture** - giving newcomers a sense of organisational norms, values, and "how things are
   done here" (formal and informal).
4. **Connection** - the interpersonal relationships and information networks the newcomer forms.

**The levels view.** Organisations operate at increasing onboarding sophistication: many do only
**Compliance** (Level 1, "Passive onboarding"); fewer add **Clarification**; the best ("Proactive
onboarding") deliberately address all Four Cs. Higher levels -> faster time-to-productivity and
better retention (drawing on the meta-analytic evidence of source 06).

**Short-term vs. long-term outcomes** of effective onboarding: self-efficacy, role clarity,
social integration, and knowledge of culture (short-term) -> satisfaction, commitment, performance,
retention, reduced turnover (long-term) - the same chain as source 06.

(Note: Bauer later evolved this to **Five Cs**, adding **Confidence**/separating Connection - the
2010 SHRM report is the Four Cs primary.)

## Up/down-rate reasoning
Moderate: practitioner guideline, but authored by the meta-analysis's lead author and grounded in
peer-reviewed evidence. Used to operationalise *what onboarding contains*; the *that-it-works*
claims rest on the peer-reviewed sources 06/08, not on this guide alone.

## Reproducibility note
The Four Cs and the passive/proactive levels are stated in the SHRM Foundation report and
reproduced in countless HR sources; the Five-Cs evolution is documented by Bauer directly.
