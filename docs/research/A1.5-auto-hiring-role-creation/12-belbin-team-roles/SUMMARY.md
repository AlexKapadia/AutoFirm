# Belbin Team Roles - role composition and the "complete the set" principle

**Question informed:** L1.A1.5 (which roles to create - team composition / gap as a missing role);
feeds L2.ORG.

## Full citation
- **Title:** Management Teams: Why They Succeed or Fail (the Team Role model)
- **Author:** R. Meredith Belbin
- **Year:** 1981 (1st ed.); 3rd ed. 2010, Butterworth-Heinemann/Routledge. Based on ~9 years of
  research at Henley Management College.
- **Venue:** Butterworth-Heinemann (book); Team Role Inventories (Belbin SPI instrument).
- **Validity review (corroborating, peer-reviewed):** Aritzeta, A., Swailes, S., & Senior, B.
  (2007). "Belbin's Team Role Model: Development, Validity and Applications for Team Building."
  Journal of Management Studies, 44(1), 96-118. https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1467-6486.2007.00666.x
- **GRADE tier:** **Moderate** for the model's structure (widely-used, but Belbin's own SPI has
  mixed psychometric support); the *concept* (complementary roles + balance) is corroborated by the
  Aritzeta et al. peer-reviewed validity review, which reports "adequate convergent validity" while
  noting mixed evidence on some role-performance links.

## What it is
A model of **nine complementary team roles** an effective team needs covered, used to diagnose
**which role is missing** from a team and to balance composition.

## Key claims (exact)
**The nine team roles** (grouped):
- *Action-oriented:* **Shaper**, **Implementer**, **Completer-Finisher**.
- *People-oriented:* **Co-ordinator**, **Teamworker**, **Resource Investigator**.
- *Thinking/cerebral:* **Plant** (creative ideas), **Monitor-Evaluator** (critical judgement),
  **Specialist** (deep expertise).

**Core principle:** a balanced team **covers the needed roles**; a team that is missing a role
(e.g. no Completer-Finisher, no Monitor-Evaluator) has a predictable, characteristic weakness.
Belbin's contribution is diagnosing teams by **role coverage**, not just individual talent.

**Empirical caveat (Aritzeta et al. 2007; also Belbin role-diversity studies):** evidence on
whether specific role mixes predict performance is **mixed**; convergent validity of the inventory
is "adequate" but role-to-performance links are not robustly established. One study found the
team-leader's Belbin role was **not** related to team performance.

## Up/down-rate reasoning
Moderate, down-rated for the contested predictive validity. Therefore AutoFirm relies on Belbin
ONLY for the qualitative **"a capability gap can be a MISSING ROLE TYPE, not just missing
headcount"** principle - which is corroborated by org-design coverage logic and RACI (a missing
accountable owner) - NOT for any quantitative role-performance claim.

## Reproducibility note
The nine roles and the coverage principle are stated in Belbin (1981/2010) and reproduced in the
Aritzeta et al. (2007) peer-reviewed review; the mixed-validity caveat is from that same review.
