## What it is
A synthesis of organizational-design science arguing that **performance depends on the FIT
between an organization's structure and its coordination needs**, which are themselves driven by
strategy, environment, and task uncertainty (the contingency/information-processing tradition).
Misfit predicts degraded performance; design is the ongoing process of re-establishing fit as
contingencies change.

## Key claims (exact)
- **Fit thesis:** organizational design seeks **fit** among design dimensions (structure,
  coordination, incentives, people) and between the organization and its **contingencies**
  (strategy, size, environment, technology). **Misfit -> lower performance.**
- **Information-processing view (Galbraith 1974):** the central design problem is matching the
  organization's **information-processing capacity** to the **information-processing requirements**
  imposed by task uncertainty. Greater uncertainty -> greater coordination/information needs ->
  the structure must add information-processing capacity (e.g. lateral roles, slack, vertical
  systems) or reduce the need.
- **Design is dynamic / continuous:** because contingencies (strategy, environment) change, fit is
  not a one-time achievement; organizations must **re-design** (re-structure roles, coordination)
  to restore fit as conditions evolve - i.e. structure follows changing strategy.
