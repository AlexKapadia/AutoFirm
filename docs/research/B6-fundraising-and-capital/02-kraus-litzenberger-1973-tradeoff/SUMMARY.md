## GRADE tier

**High** — peer-reviewed in The Journal of Finance, the foundational formalization of the trade-off
theory in a state-preference framework. Widely cited as the canonical origin. No down-rate.

## Key claims

- **Optimal capital structure exists** and is found by **balancing two opposing forces**:
  1. the **tax advantage of debt** (interest tax shield, from MM 1963), which increases firm value
     with leverage, against
  2. the **deadweight costs of bankruptcy / financial distress**, which rise as leverage rises
     (higher fixed interest obligations raise the probability and expected cost of distress).
- The firm sets a **target leverage ratio** where the marginal tax benefit of an extra dollar of
  debt equals the marginal expected bankruptcy cost. Conceptually:
  > V_L = V_U + PV(interest tax shield) - PV(financial distress costs)

  (the third term is the trade-off addition to MM's V_L = V_U + T_C*D).
- This resolves the MM-1963 "100% debt" puzzle: distress costs cap leverage well below 100%.

## Variants noted (for the option-space survey)

- **Static trade-off:** firm targets a fixed optimal ratio.
- **Dynamic trade-off:** firms adjust toward a target with adjustment costs (Fischer-Heinkel-Zechner
  1989), explaining why observed leverage fluctuates around a target.

## Reproducibility note

The state-preference derivation yields an interior optimum once a positive bankruptcy penalty is
added to the MM tax framework; the trade-off intuition above is the standard textbook statement and
is corroborated empirically by source 04.
