## GRADE tier

**Moderate** — recognized industry **primary data** (Carta administers cap tables for tens of
thousands of US startups, so these are observed transactions, not survey opinion). Down-rate one
notch vs. peer-reviewed: it is a single vendor's book of business (US-centric, Carta-customer
selection bias) and figures are time-varying. Numbers must be cited **with their as-of date** and
treated as a moving benchmark, not a constant.

## Key quantitative claims (with as-of dates; benchmarks, not constants)

- **Instrument mix (early stage):** SAFEs are the dominant pre-seed instrument (used in 70%+ of
  pre-seed deals). Of US startup SAFEs in **2024**: **61% cap-only, 30% cap + discount, 8%
  discount-only, 1% neither**.
- **Stage benchmarks (2025, medians):**
  - Pre-seed: round ~$0.75M-$1.5M; post-money SAFE caps ~$10M (rounds $250K-$1M) to ~$15M
    (rounds $1M-$2.5M).
  - Seed: post-money valuation reached an all-time-high median of **$24M in Q4 2025** (up from $18M
    a year earlier); ~20-25% dilution.
  - Series A: median post-money **$78.7M in Q4 2025** (up 37% YoY from $57.5M); Q1 2025 median
    dilution **17.9%** (down from 20.9% a year earlier).
- **Dilution trend:** median dilution across seed-through-Series-C fell from ~18% to ~16% over the
  trailing year.
- **Market volume:** Carta startups raised ~$120B in 2025, up ~17% YoY.

## Why it matters here

Supplies the **empirical "stage norms" numbers** the playbook needs to sanity-check a proposed raise
(is the ask / valuation / dilution within market range for the stage?) and to model **dilution per
round**. These are *defaults to display with their date*, never magic constants baked into logic.

## Reproducibility note

Each figure is sourced to a specific Carta report and quarter above; a reviewer re-fetches the named
report and confirms the median for that quarter. Values WILL drift — the playbook must re-pull, not
hard-code.
