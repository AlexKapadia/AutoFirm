# SUMMARY — Berger & Udell (1998): The financial growth cycle of small firms

**Informs ontology question(s):** L1.B6.1 (stage-to-capital mapping; "stage norms") -> feeds L2.B6.

## Full citation

- **Primary:** Berger, A. N. & Udell, G. F. (1998). *The Economics of Small Business Finance: The
  Roles of Private Equity and Debt Markets in the Financial Growth Cycle.* **Journal of Banking &
  Finance**, 22(6-8): 613-673. (Elsevier.)
  RePEc: https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jbfina/v22y1998i6-8p613-673.html ·
  Fed FEDS 1998-15: https://www.federalreserve.gov/econres/feds/the-economics-of-small-business-finance-the-roles-of-private-equity-and-debt-markets-in-the-financial-growth-cycle.htm

## GRADE tier

**High** — peer-reviewed in a top finance journal (JBF) and issued as a Federal Reserve FEDS working
paper; the canonical "financial growth cycle" reference for small-firm finance. Anchor for the idea
that **different capital structures are optimal at different stages**.

## Key claims

- **Financial growth cycle paradigm:** "different capital structures are optimal at different points
  in the cycle." As a firm grows in **size, age, and information availability**, its menu of viable
  funding sources widens.
- **Stage -> source mapping (the load-bearing contribution):**
  - **Infant / opaque, no track record:** insider finance (founder, friends/family), trade credit,
    angel finance. Information opacity blocks arm's-length debt and public markets.
  - **Adolescent / some track record:** venture capital, mid-term financing, intermediated debt
    (banks) as collateral and track record appear.
  - **Mature / informationally transparent:** public equity, public debt, commercial paper — the
    full arm's-length menu.
- **Information asymmetry is the binding constraint** for young firms (links directly to the
  pecking-order mechanism, source 03): the more opaque the firm, the more it relies on insider and
  relationship-based finance rather than arm's-length securities.

## Why it matters here

It is the academic backbone for "**stage norms**": AutoFirm's playbook must condition the financing
recommendation on the **firm's life-cycle position** (opacity/age/size), not apply one capital
structure universally. This is the generality hook for B12 — the cycle applies across industries,
though the *speed* through it differs (capital-light SaaS vs. asset-heavy manufacturing).

## Reproducibility note

The growth-cycle table mapping firm age/size/opacity to funding sources is the paper's central
exhibit and is reproduced in standard entrepreneurial-finance texts; the qualitative claims above
match that exhibit.
