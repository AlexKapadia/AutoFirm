## Key claims (with as-of dates)

**Grants (SBIR/STTR — non-dilutive, US):**
- Programs provide **>$4B/year** in non-dilutive funding to US small businesses.
- Federal guideline caps (**as of April 2026**): Phase I up to **$323,090**; Phase II up to
  **$2,153,927** (without SBA approval). Agency-specific: NSF Phase I up to $305K / Phase II up to
  $1.25M; NIH Phase I up to ~$293K / Phase II up to ~$1.96M.
- **Phase I = feasibility; Phase II = full development.** Non-dilutive: no equity given up.

**Revenue-based financing (RBF — non-dilutive debt-like):**
- Capital repaid as a **percentage of future (often recurring) revenue (ARR/MRR)**; payments **flex
  with cash flow** (more when revenue is up, less when down). No fixed amortization; **preserves the
  cap table entirely** (no equity, no warrants typically). Funded in **weeks, not months**. Best for
  companies with predictable recurring revenue.

**Venture debt (debt with an equity kicker):**
- Operates like a **traditional loan: fixed term + interest**. Considered "non-dilutive" vs. an
  equity round but lenders typically require **warrants** -> small dilution, **~0.1%-1.0%**.
- Typically requires **demonstrated revenue traction + institutional VC backing**; suits **Series A
  and later**. Used to **extend runway between equity rounds** without an immediate down-round.

## Why it matters here

Completes the **full instrument menu** beyond equity: (1) **grants** (non-dilutive, milestone/
R&D-gated, slow, eligibility-constrained), (2) **RBF** (non-dilutive, revenue-contingent, fast,
recurring-revenue-gated), (3) **venture debt** (low-dilution, runway extension, requires traction +
VC backing). The playbook must select among these by **firm stage, revenue profile, and dilution
tolerance** — not default to equity.
