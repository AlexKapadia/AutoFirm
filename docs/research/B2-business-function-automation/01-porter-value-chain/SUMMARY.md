## Key claims (faithful to source)

**Definition.** A firm's *value chain* disaggregates the firm into its strategically relevant activities to understand cost behaviour and sources of differentiation. The chain comprises **value activities** (physically and technologically distinct activities) plus **margin** (total value minus the collective cost of performing the value activities).

### Primary activities (5) — creation, sale, transfer, and after-sale support
1. **Inbound logistics** — receiving, storing, disseminating inputs (material handling, warehousing, inventory control, returns to suppliers).
2. **Operations** — transforming inputs into the final product (machining, packaging, assembly, equipment maintenance, testing).
3. **Outbound logistics** — collecting, storing, distributing the product to buyers (finished-goods warehousing, order processing, scheduling, delivery).
4. **Marketing & Sales** — means by which buyers can buy and inducement to do so (advertising, promotion, sales force, channel selection, pricing).
5. **Service** — enhancing/maintaining product value (installation, repair, training, parts supply, product adjustment).

### Support activities (4) — underpin the primary activities and each other
1. **Procurement** — the *function* of purchasing inputs (not the purchased inputs themselves); occurs across the whole chain.
2. **Technology development** — know-how, procedures, technology in process or product (R&D, product/process design, automation engineering).
3. **Human resource management** — recruiting, hiring, training, development, compensation of all personnel.
4. **Firm infrastructure** — general management, planning, finance, accounting, legal, government affairs, quality management; supports the entire chain.

**Direct / indirect / quality-assurance** sub-distinction applies within each category (e.g. maintenance and scheduling are *indirect* enablers).

**Linkages.** Value activities are connected by *linkages* — how one activity is performed affects cost/performance of another, within the chain and across suppliers'/channels' chains (the "value system"). Coordinating linkages is a source of advantage.
