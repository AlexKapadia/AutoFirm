# SUMMARY — Frey & Osborne, "The Future of Employment"

## Full citation
- **Title:** *The Future of Employment: How Susceptible Are Jobs to Computerisation?*
- **Authors:** Carl Benedikt Frey & Michael A. Osborne
- **Year:** Oxford Martin School working paper 2013; **published 2017**.
- **Venue:** *Technological Forecasting and Social Change*, **Vol. 114 (2017), pp. 254–280**. DOI: 10.1016/j.techfore.2016.08.019
- **URLs:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0040162516302244 ; working-paper PDF: https://oms-www.files.svdcdn.com/production/downloads/academic/The_Future_of_Employment.pdf

## Questions informed
- **L1.B2.3** Which functions are automatable to what degree — occupation-level automation risk.

## GRADE tier
- **High** — peer-reviewed journal (TFSC), the seminal and most-cited quantitative study of automation risk. Down-rate consideration (recorded): *occupation-based* method criticized for overestimation (see source 07 Arntz/OECD); the headline 47% must therefore be reported WITH its critique, not alone.

## Key claims (faithful to source)

**Method.** A **Gaussian process classifier** was trained on **702 detailed occupations** (O*NET).
Researchers hand-labelled 70 occupations as automatable (1) or not (0), then used **nine O*NET
variables** as features for three **engineering bottleneck** categories, to estimate each
occupation's **probability of computerisation**.

**The three bottlenecks and their nine O*NET variables (reproduced exactly):**
1. **Perception and Manipulation** — *Finger Dexterity; Manual Dexterity; Cramped Work Space, Awkward Positions.*
2. **Creative Intelligence** — *Originality; Fine Arts.*
3. **Social Intelligence** — *Social Perceptiveness; Negotiation; Persuasion; Assisting and Caring for Others.*

**Headline finding (exact):** "**about 47 percent of total US employment is at risk**" — i.e. in
the **high-risk** category. Risk bins by probability of computerisation:
- **High risk:** probability **> 0.7**
- **Medium risk:** **0.3 – 0.7**
- **Low risk:** **< 0.7** … (paper: low = below 0.3; medium = 0.3–0.7; high = above 0.7).

**Secondary finding:** wages and educational attainment exhibit a **strong negative relationship**
with an occupation's probability of computerisation.

## Reproducibility note
702 occupations, Gaussian process classifier, 9 O*NET variables across 3 bottlenecks, 47% high-risk (>0.7), are all stated in Frey & Osborne (2017) §3–§5 and Table (occupation rankings). Confirmable in the TFSC published version and the Oxford Martin working paper PDF.
