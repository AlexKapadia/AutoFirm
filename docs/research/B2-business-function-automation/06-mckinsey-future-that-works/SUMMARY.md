## Questions informed
- **L1.B2.3** Which functions automate to what degree — the canonical *task/activity-based* feasibility map.

## GRADE tier
- **Moderate-to-High** — primary research report from a recognized institute, large dataset (~2,000 work activities across 800+ occupations, US + global). Not academic peer-review (down-rate), but methodologically transparent, widely cited, and corroborated in direction by Arntz/OECD and Autor-Levy-Murnane (up-rate for consistency).

## Key claims (faithful to source, exact numbers)

**Unit of analysis:** *work activities*, not whole jobs. MGI decomposed occupations into ~2,000
activities and mapped each to **18 capabilities** in **5 groups**: *sensory perception; cognitive
capabilities; natural-language processing; social & emotional capabilities; physical capabilities.*

**Seven top-level activity types and technical automation potential (exact, current tech):**
| Activity type | Technical automation potential |
|---|---|
| Predictable physical work | **81%** |
| Processing data | **69%** |
| Collecting data | **64%** |
| Unpredictable physical work | **25%** (≈) |
| Stakeholder interactions | **20%** (≈, "less than 20%") |
| Applying expertise (decision-making, planning, creative) | **18%** (≈, "less than 20%") |
| Managing others | **9%** (≈, lowest) |

(Activities needing high social/cognitive skill: "often less than 20% automation potential.")

**Headline findings (exact):**
- "**about 50% of all work activities** could theoretically be automated" by adapting currently
  demonstrated technology (other MGI summaries state "~half" / 49%).
- "**Less than 5%** of all current jobs could be **fully** automated."
- "**Around 60%** of [all occupations] have **at least 30%** of their constituent activities that
  could be automated."

## Reproducibility note

Confirmable in the full-report PDF and the executive summary.
