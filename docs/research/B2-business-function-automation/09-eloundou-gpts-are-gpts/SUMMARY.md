# SUMMARY — Eloundou et al., "GPTs are GPTs"

## Full citation
- **Title:** *GPTs are GPTs: An Early Look at the Labor Market Impact Potential of Large Language Models*
- **Authors:** Tyna Eloundou, Sam Manning, Pamela Mishkin, Daniel Rock
- **Year:** 2023 (revised 2023; later published in *Science*, 2024, as "GPTs are GPTs: Labor market impact potential of LLMs", Science 384:6702)
- **Venue:** arXiv:2303.10130 (working paper); *Science* (2024). URLs: https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.10130 ; https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2303.10130

## Questions informed
- **L1.B2.3** Which functions automate to what degree — the **LLM-era** update that supersedes the 2003–2017 priors for cognitive/language tasks.

## GRADE tier
- **High** — methodologically transparent, large rubric-based study; subsequently peer-reviewed in *Science* (2024). The definitive early quantification of LLM task exposure. Down-rate: authors affiliated with OpenAI (conflict-of-interest noted); up-rate: human + GPT-4 dual rating, broadly corroborated (ILO, Bruegel).

## Key claims (faithful to source, exact)

**Method.** A **task- and activity-level** rubric over O*NET, rated by **both human experts and
GPT-4**, scoring each task's *exposure* (whether LLM access reduces time-to-complete by ≥50%).

**Exposure rubric (E0/E1/E2), reproduced:**
- **E0** — no exposure: LLM access gives no or minimal (<50%) time reduction.
- **E1** — direct exposure: an LLM via an interface (e.g. ChatGPT) could reduce task time by ≥50%.
- **E2** — LLM+-exposed: an LLM *alone* won't halve the time, but **additional software built on
  top of an LLM** could reduce time-to-complete by ≥50%.

**Three exposure measures, reproduced:**
- **α (alpha)** = share where E1 (direct only) — lower bound.
- **β (beta)** = E1 + 0.5·E2 — mid estimate (the 0.5 weights E2 for the extra investment needed).
- **ζ (zeta)** = E1 + E2 — upper bound (maximal exposure incl. LLM-powered software).

**Headline findings (exact):**
- "**Around 80% of the U.S. workforce** could have at least **10%** of their work tasks affected"
  by LLM introduction (α threshold).
- "Approximately **19% of workers** may see at least **50%** of their tasks impacted" (β measure).
- Accounting for LLM-powered software (ζ): up to **~49%** of workers could have ≥50% of tasks exposed.
- **Most exposed:** writing, programming, mathematical reasoning, translation, data analysis.
  **Least exposed:** tasks requiring science/critical-thinking, physical labour, manual dexterity,
  outdoor work.

## Reproducibility note
The E0/E1/E2 rubric, α/β/ζ measures (β = E1 + 0.5·E2), and the 80%-at-≥10% / 19%-at-≥50% / ~49% upper-bound figures are reproduced from Eloundou et al. (2023), §3 (rubric) and §4 (results). Confirmable in the arXiv:2303.10130 v2 PDF and the 2024 Science article.
