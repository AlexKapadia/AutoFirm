# SUMMARY — eTOM (TM Forum) & SCOR (ASCM) — industry-specific process frameworks

## Full citations
- **eTOM / Business Process Framework:** TM Forum, *GB921 Business Process Framework (eTOM) Suite* (v24.x). Originally "enhanced Telecom Operations Map." TM Forum, Morristown NJ. https://www.tmforum.org/open-digital-architecture/process-framework-etom/ ; overview: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Business_Process_Framework_(eTOM)
- **SCOR (Supply Chain Operations Reference model):** Association for Supply Chain Management (ASCM, formerly APICS / Supply-Chain Council). https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/computer-science/supply-chain-council

## Questions informed
- **L1.B2.1** — these are the *industry-specific* alternatives to a generic taxonomy (the
  alternative-space survey, DEPTH-RUBRIC §4): they show how the canonical taxonomy specializes.

## GRADE tier
- **Moderate** — recognized industry-standard frameworks maintained by standards bodies (TM Forum, ASCM), broadly adopted in telecom and supply-chain respectively. Not peer-reviewed; vendor/consortium-maintained. Used here for *coverage of the alternative space*, not as a relied-upon quantitative claim.

## Key claims (faithful to source)

**eTOM (Business Process Framework).**
- A multi-layered, industry-agreed view of the key business processes to run a digital enterprise,
  built by/for telecom service providers; part of TM Forum's Open Digital Architecture.
- Hierarchical, decomposed across **5 levels** (Level 0 conceptual → Level 4 detail).
- Three top-level process areas (Level 0): **Strategy, Infrastructure & Product (SIP)**;
  **Operations (OPS)**; **Enterprise Management**. Operations is structured by the
  "FAB" end-to-end groupings (Fulfillment, Assurance, Billing).

**SCOR (Supply Chain Operations Reference).**
- A cross-industry *supply-chain* reference + diagnostic model.
- Defines the supply chain as **five primary management processes: Plan, Source, Make, Deliver,
  Return** (later versions add **Enable**).
- Decomposed across **4 levels:** Level 1 (top/process types), Level 2 (configuration),
  Level 3 (process element), Level 4 (implementation).
- Pairs processes with standard **performance metrics** (reliability, responsiveness, agility,
  cost, asset-management efficiency) — a built-in measurement layer.

## Reproducibility note
eTOM's 3 Level-0 areas + 5 levels and SCOR's 5(+1) processes + 4 levels are stable, documented structures (TM Forum GB921; ASCM SCOR). A reviewer can confirm against the GB921 suite and the ASCM SCOR Digital Standard.
