## Key claims (faithful to source)

**Structure.** The PCF is a hierarchical taxonomy with up to **5 levels**:
Level 1 = **Category**, Level 2 = **Process Group**, Level 3 = **Process**, Level 4 = **Activity**,
Level 5 = **Task**. The cross-industry PCF contains **hundreds of processes** with attached
definitions.

**The 12 top-level Categories** — 5 *operating* + 7 *management & support*:

*Operating processes (5):*
1. Develop Vision and Strategy
2. Develop and Manage Products and Services
3. Market and Sell Products and Services
4. Deliver Physical Products *(and "Deliver Services" — split in later versions)*
5. Manage Customer Service

*Management & support processes (7):*
6. Develop and Manage Human Capital
7. Manage Information Technology (IT)
8. Manage Financial Resources
9. Acquire, Construct, and Manage Assets
10. Manage Enterprise Risk, Compliance, Remediation, and Resiliency
11. Manage External Relationships
12. Develop and Manage Business Capabilities

> Note: the exact wording/ordering and the operating-vs-support split evolves slightly across versions (early versions described "five operating + seven support" categories; v7+ refined the labels). The 12-category, 5-level structure is stable across versions.

**Purpose.** A common, industry-neutral vocabulary to *name, organize, map, and benchmark*
processes "in terms of horizontal processes rather than vertical functions" — letting any
organization, in any industry, compare performance internally and externally.

## Reproducibility note
The "12 categories (5 operating + 7 support), 5-level hierarchy, hundreds of processes" structure is stated on APQC's framework pages and reproduced in every cross-industry PCF release. A reviewer can download the v8.0 cross-industry PDF/Excel from APQC's resource library and confirm the category list and level definitions on page 1.
