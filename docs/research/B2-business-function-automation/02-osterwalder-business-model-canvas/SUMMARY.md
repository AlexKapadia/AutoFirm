## Key claims (faithful to source)

**Definition.** A business model "describes the rationale of how an organization creates, delivers, and captures value." The Canvas decomposes it into **nine building blocks** grouped into four pillars.

### The nine building blocks
1. **Customer Segments (CS)** — the different groups of people/organizations the firm serves.
2. **Value Propositions (VP)** — the bundle of products/services creating value for a segment.
3. **Channels (CH)** — how the firm communicates with and reaches segments to deliver the VP.
4. **Customer Relationships (CR)** — the types of relationships established with each segment.
5. **Revenue Streams (RS)** — the cash a firm generates from each segment.
6. **Key Resources (KR)** — the most important assets required (physical, intellectual, human, financial).
7. **Key Activities (KA)** — the most important things a firm must do to make the model work.
8. **Key Partnerships (KP)** — the network of suppliers/partners that make the model work.
9. **Cost Structure (CS$)** — all costs incurred to operate the model.

### Four pillars (groupings)
- **Offering:** Value Propositions.
- **Customers (front-stage):** Customer Segments, Channels, Customer Relationships.
- **Infrastructure (back-stage):** Key Resources, Key Activities, Key Partnerships.
- **Financial viability:** Revenue Streams, Cost Structure.

**Key Activities categories** (source): *Production*, *Problem-solving*, and *Platform/Network*.
**Key Resources categories:** *Physical, Intellectual, Human, Financial*.
