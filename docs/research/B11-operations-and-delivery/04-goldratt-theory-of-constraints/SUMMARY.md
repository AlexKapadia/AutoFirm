## Key claims (faithful)

### Core reframe
A system's output is limited by its **constraint** (bottleneck). Improving a non-constraint does NOT
improve system **throughput** — only the constraint governs the rate of value generation. Local
efficiencies ≠ global performance.

### The Five Focusing Steps (exact)
1. **IDENTIFY** the system's constraint.
2. **EXPLOIT** the constraint (get the most from it without major new investment).
3. **SUBORDINATE** everything else to the above decision (other resources serve the constraint).
4. **ELEVATE** the constraint (increase its capacity, e.g. invest, add resource).
5. If in a previous step the constraint is broken, **GO BACK to step 1** — and do not let **inertia**
   become the constraint (continuous improvement; the constraint moves).

### Drum-Buffer-Rope (DBR)
- **Drum** — the constraint sets the pace/drumbeat for the whole system.
- **Buffer** — time/inventory buffer placed before the constraint to protect it from upstream
  variation (so it never starves).
- **Rope** — a release mechanism that ties material release into the system to the constraint's pace
  (limits WIP to what the constraint can consume).

### Throughput Accounting (3 measures)
- **Throughput (T)** = rate at which the system generates money through sales =
  **sales revenue − truly variable (unit-level) cost**.
- **Inventory / Investment (I)** = money tied up in things the system intends to sell (incl. WIP, assets).
- **Operating Expense (OE)** = money spent turning Inventory into Throughput.
- Goal: **increase T** while reducing I and OE — T prioritized (unbounded above), vs cost-cutting
  (bounded below by zero).
