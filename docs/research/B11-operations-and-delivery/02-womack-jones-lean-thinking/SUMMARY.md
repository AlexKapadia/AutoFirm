## GRADE tier
- **High** for the five-principle framework: this is the primary, canonical Western codification of
  lean, corroborated by LEI (the authors' own institute) and ubiquitous independent OM literature.
- **Down-rate:** efficacy claims in the book are case-study/illustrative, not RCT — treat specific
  improvement % as supporting, not safety-critical.

## Key claims (faithful)

### The five principles of lean (exact)
1. **Value** — "Specify value from the standpoint of the end customer" by product family. Value is
   defined by the customer, not the producer.
2. **Value stream** — "Identify all the steps in the value stream for each product family,
   eliminating wherever possible those steps that do not create value."
3. **Flow** — "Make the value-creating steps occur in tight sequence so the product flows smoothly
   toward the customer." (Single-piece flow over batch-and-queue.)
4. **Pull** — "Let customers pull value from the next upstream activity" — produce only what is
   pulled by downstream demand (no overproduction).
5. **Perfection** — continuously improve so that value flows with zero waste; the four prior
   principles interact in a virtuous loop (kaizen).

### Three classes of activity (value-stream mapping)
When mapping a value stream, each step is one of:
- **Value-adding** (the customer would pay for it),
- **Type-1 muda** — non-value-adding but currently unavoidable (e.g. some inspection),
- **Type-2 muda** — non-value-adding and immediately removable.

### Relationship to TPS
Lean = the Western generalization of TPS (01-ohno) beyond automotive manufacturing, explicitly
extended by the authors to services and any value-creating process.
