## Key claims (faithful)

### What a service blueprint is
A **service blueprint** is a visual map of a service process that depicts BOTH customer-visible
(front-stage) and customer-invisible (back-stage) activities and their interconnections, separated by
defined **lines**. It makes an intangible service process explicit and analyzable.

### The blueprint's lines / swim-lanes (standard formalization, Bitner/Ostrom/Morgan 2008)
1. **Physical evidence** (tangibles the customer encounters).
2. **Customer actions.**
3. **Line of interaction** — separates customer from the provider.
4. **Onstage / visible contact-employee actions** (front-stage).
5. **Line of visibility** — separates what the customer sees from what they don't.
6. **Backstage / invisible contact-employee actions.**
7. **Line of internal interaction** — separates contact staff from support.
8. **Support processes** (systems/other functions enabling the service).

### Key analytical uses (Shostack 1984)
- **Identify fail points** — steps where the service can break down (design countermeasures there).
- **Establish time/tolerance standards** for execution (the blueprint carries timing).
- **Analyze profitability** as a function of execution time/variability.
