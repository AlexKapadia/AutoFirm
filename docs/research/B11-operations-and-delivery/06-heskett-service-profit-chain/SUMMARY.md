## Key claims (faithful)

### The chain (causal links, in order)
The Service-Profit Chain links **operations → employees → customers → profit** as a causal chain:
1. **Internal service quality** (workplace/tools/job design) → drives **employee satisfaction**.
2. **Employee satisfaction** → drives **employee retention & productivity**.
3. Employee retention/productivity → **external service value** (value delivered to customers).
4. External service value → **customer satisfaction**.
5. **Customer satisfaction** → **customer loyalty** (retention, repeat, referral).
6. **Customer loyalty** → **revenue growth and profitability**.

> Headline relationships (as stated by the authors): satisfied, loyal, productive **employees**
> create **value**; that value drives customer **satisfaction → loyalty → profit**. Customer loyalty
> (not mere satisfaction) is the primary driver of profitability; very-satisfied customers are far
> more loyal than merely-satisfied ones.

### Operational implication
Profit in services is driven from the *operations and people* end, not just the marketing end —
investments in internal service quality propagate measurably to the bottom line.
