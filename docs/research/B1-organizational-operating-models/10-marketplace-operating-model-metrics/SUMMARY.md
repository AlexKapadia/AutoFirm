## Key constructs & metrics (definitions)
- **GMV (Gross Merchandise Value):** total value of transactions flowing through the marketplace in
  a period. A *volume* metric, NOT revenue.
- **Take Rate (%):** `Platform revenue / GMV` — the share of GMV the platform captures. Category-
  dependent (low for high-frequency commodity flows; higher for high-trust/high-service categories).
- **Liquidity:** the *single most critical* marketplace health metric — the probability that a
  listing (supply) or a search (demand) results in a transaction within a reasonable time. Measured
  e.g. as match rate / % of listings sold / % of searches converting.
- **The cold-start problem:** neither side joins until the other is present. Solved by deliberate
  seeding, niche/geographic concentration, subsidizing the harder side, single-player tools.
- **Network effects:** value rises as participants grow; the defensive moat justifying premium
  valuation even at modest current financials.
- **"5 critical forces" (Liquidity Forces Map):** supply-side acquisition cost, demand-side
  acquisition cost, match rate, repeat-transaction rate, take-rate elasticity.
