# BEST-PARTS — Marketplace Operating-Model Metrics

## What AutoFirm ADOPTS

1. **Liquidity-first operating model for any two-sided business (panel row 5).** When AutoFirm
   builds/operates a marketplace, the operating model is organized around **liquidity** as the North
   Star metric, with GMV and take rate secondary. The 5 critical forces (supply/demand CAC, match
   rate, repeat rate, take-rate elasticity) become the standing KPI contract.
   - **Build implication:** L2.B4 models marketplace unit economics from liquidity + take rate (not
     GMV alone — GMV is a vanity metric); L2.B7/B8 split acquisition into two distinct supply-side
     and demand-side motions.

2. **The cold-start playbook is a reusable sequencing rule.** "Build the harder side first;
   concentrate geographically/niche; subsidize the constrained side; consider a single-player tool."
   This is a concrete, industry-parameterized launch sequence AutoFirm can execute for marketplace
   clients — a deterministic decision tree, not improvisation.

3. **Take rate as a pricing decision tied to delivered value (links to L2.B5).** Take rate should be
   set to category norms and raised only as value/liquidity is proven — a rule, not a guess.

## What AutoFirm REJECTS / caution
- **Reject GMV as a success metric.** GMV is transaction *volume*, not revenue or health; optimizing
  it invites subsidy-fueled vanity growth. AutoFirm reports GMV but gates decisions on liquidity +
  take rate + unit economics.
- **Reject applying the marketplace model to non-network businesses.** Network-effect reasoning only
  applies where cross-side value genuinely compounds (panel row 5; partially fintech/healthcare
  platforms). Forcing it elsewhere overfits.
- Caution: network-effect "moat" claims are easy to over-assert; require evidence of actual
  cross-side dependency before invoking premium-valuation logic.

## Quantification for evidence/
- A marketplace KPI panel: liquidity (match rate), GMV, take rate, supply CAC, demand CAC, repeat
  rate — computable for a real public marketplace's disclosed figures (§3.12 public-data validation).
- A cold-start simulation chart: liquidity vs. time under "seed harder side first" vs. naive
  balanced-spend — demonstrates the playbook's value quantitatively.
