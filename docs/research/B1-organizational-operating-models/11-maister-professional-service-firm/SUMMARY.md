## GRADE tier
**Moderate-to-High.** The canonical, originator-authored treatise on PSF management; the 1982 SMR
article is peer-reviewed primary. Concepts (leverage, project types, utilization, realization) are
universally adopted in the services literature. Down-rated one tier for book (vs. RCT) form; the
core constructs are exactly stated and cross-corroborated.

## Key constructs (definitions, faithful)
- **Leverage:** the ratio (mix) of **junior : middle : senior** staff in the firm. *"The required
  shape of the organization is primarily determined by the skill requirements of its work."* Matching
  the staffing mix to the work's skill mix is "central to keeping the firm in balance."
- **The three project types (and their leverage):**
  - **Brains** — frontier/highly complex problems; creativity-led; **low leverage** (few juniors per
    senior; senior-heavy).
  - **Grey Hair** — customized but less novel; experience-led; **medium leverage.**
  - **Procedure** — well-recognized, programmatic problems; **high leverage** (many juniors per
    senior).
- **Mismatch costs (exact):** too much procedural work -> expensive seniors do low-value tasks; too
  much Brains work -> staff shortages and quality/risk problems. (A services analogue of misfit cost,
  source 03.)
- **Operating KPIs (services):**
  - **Utilization rate** = `billable hours / available hours` (e.g. 30 billed / 40 available = 75%).
  - **Realization rate** = `billed-and-collected hours (or $) / billable hours worked` (e.g. 900 /
    1000 = 90%).
  - **Effective billing rate**, **revenue per professional**, **leverage ratio** — combined, these
    drive PSF profitability. (Profit per partner ~ margin x productivity x leverage.)

## Reproducibility note
Leverage, the Brains/Grey-Hair/Procedure typology, and utilization/realization definitions are
reproduced consistently across Maister (1982 SMR; 1993 book) and the modern PSF-KPI literature. A
reviewer can re-derive from Maister's 1982 SMR article.
