## Foundational definition (verbatim, Mintzberg 1979)
*"The structure of an organization can be defined simply as the sum total of the ways in which it
divides its labor into distinct tasks and then achieves coordination among them."*

## The five (later six) coordinating mechanisms
1. **Mutual adjustment** — coordination by informal communication between operators.
2. **Direct supervision** — one person issues orders/monitors others.
3. **Standardization of work processes** — the content of work is specified/programmed.
4. **Standardization of outputs** — results (e.g. dimensions, performance) are specified.
5. **Standardization of skills (and knowledge)** — the training of the worker is specified.
   (Mintzberg later added **standardization of norms** — shared beliefs.)

## The five basic parts of the organization
**Strategic apex** (top management) · **Middle line** (managers linking apex to core) ·
**Operating core** (workers doing the basic work) · **Technostructure** (analysts who standardize
work) · **Support staff** (indirect services). (Plus **ideology/culture** as a sixth element.)

## The five structural configurations (verbatim mapping)
| Configuration | Prime coordinating mechanism | Key part | Type of decentralization |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Simple Structure** | Direct supervision | Strategic apex | Vertical & horizontal centralization |
| **Machine Bureaucracy** | Standardization of work processes | Technostructure | Limited horizontal decentralization |
| **Professional Bureaucracy** | Standardization of skills | Operating core | Vertical & horizontal decentralization |
| **Divisionalized Form** | Standardization of outputs | Middle line | Limited vertical decentralization |
| **Adhocracy** | Mutual adjustment | Support staff (and/or operating core) | Selective decentralization |

## Reproducibility note
The five mechanisms, five parts, and the configuration->mechanism->key-part->decentralization
mapping are reproduced consistently across Lunenburg (2012, peer-reviewed) and multiple independent
practitioner summaries of Mintzberg (1979/1983); a reviewer can re-derive from either primary book.
