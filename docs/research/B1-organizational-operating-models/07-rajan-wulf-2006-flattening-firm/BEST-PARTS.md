# BEST-PARTS — Rajan & Wulf (2006)

## What AutoFirm ADOPTS

1. **Empirical license to FLATTEN: better information systems -> wider spans + fewer levels.**
   Rajan & Wulf show that when knowledge is disseminated (IT), firms safely widen CEO span (4->7)
   and cut levels (-25%). AutoFirm's "vertical information system" is its **append-only audit log +
   externalized state (git/task-list)**; because that channel is excellent, AutoFirm can run
   *flatter* agent orgs than Graicunas's 1933 math would suggest — more direct subagents per
   orchestrator, fewer middle layers — *provided* the information system is strong.
   - **Build implication:** the span cap (source 06) is **raised** when standardized-output
     contracts + a high-quality audit/state channel are in place; lowered when they are not. The
     org engine couples span to information-system quality, exactly as the data show.

2. **Delegation is real only when authority + incentive move together.** The finding that pay/
   incentives followed managers toward the CEO means delegation must be *genuine*: a delegated agent
   gets real decision scope and is measured on real outcomes, not nominal.
   - **Build implication:** when AutoFirm delegates to a subagent/sub-orchestrator, it delegates the
     *decision* (not just legwork) and assigns it an owned objective/metric (the "reward" of the
     Star, source 02) — matching CLAUDE.md §3.1 "owns the decision."

3. **Eliminate redundant middle layers (the COO-decline lesson).** Flattening came partly from
   removing an intermediary. AutoFirm should avoid gratuitous sub-orchestrator layers when the
   information system can carry the span directly — keep the hierarchy as shallow as the
   coordination load allows (no graveyard layers, CLAUDE.md §3.8).

## What AutoFirm REJECTS / caution
- **Reject "flatter is always better."** The data are conditional on strong information systems and
  knowledgeable lower levels. With weak contracts or high task uncertainty, flattening overloads the
  apex (Graicunas explosion). AutoFirm flattens *only* when the enabling conditions hold.
- **Indirectness caveat:** the dataset is 1986-2003 large US human firms. The *direction* (better IS
  -> safe flattening) is the transferable invariant; the literal numbers (7 reports) are NOT a cap
  for agents — AutoFirm must set its own cap empirically (source 06 ablation).

- Track AutoFirm's own hierarchy **depth** and **mean span** over a run as evidence the engine
