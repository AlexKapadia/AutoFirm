# SUMMARY — EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689) + cross-sector regulatory map

## Full citations
- **Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 13 June 2024** laying
  down harmonised rules on artificial intelligence (Artificial Intelligence Act). Official Journal of
  the EU, published 12 July 2024; in force 1 August 2024.
  Primary: EUR-Lex CELEX 32024R1689 (https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj).
- **U.S. sector regulations (primary statutes), as a cross-sector map:** **HIPAA** (Pub. L. 104-191,
  45 CFR Parts 160/164 — healthcare PHI); **GLBA** (Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act, 15 U.S.C. §6801 et seq. —
  financial data); **BSA/AML + KYC** (31 U.S.C. §5311 et seq.; FinCEN CDD Rule); **PCI DSS** (card data,
  industry standard); **GDPR** (Regulation (EU) 2016/679 — EU data protection); **CCPA/CPRA**
  (Cal. Civ. Code §1798.100 et seq. — California privacy).
- **Corroborating professional surveys:** Scrut.io *Fintech Compliance*; CertPro *Compliance
  Regulations by Industry*; Usercentrics *Global Data Privacy Laws 2025 Guide*.

## Key claims (with locators)
1. **EU AI Act risk tiers** (Reg. 2024/1689, Title II-III): a **four-tier risk-based** framework —
   **(1) Unacceptable risk → PROHIBITED** (Art. 5, banned outright, no compliance path); **(2) High
   risk** (Annex III + Art. 6) → permitted but subject to **conformity assessment + Chapter 2 (Arts.
   8-15) requirements** before market placement; **(3) Limited risk** → transparency obligations;
   **(4) Minimal risk** → largely unregulated.
2. **GPAI models** (Reg. 2024/1689, Title V): general-purpose AI models carry their own obligations
   (transparency, copyright, systemic-risk for the largest models).
3. **Timeline:** in force **1 Aug 2024**; **prohibited-practice + AI-literacy** rules applied **2 Feb
   2025**; **GPAI** obligations **2 Aug 2025**; **remaining provisions 2 Aug 2026**; high-risk-in-
   regulated-products deferred to **2 Aug 2027**.
4. **Penalties** (Art. 99): up to **EUR 35,000,000 or 7% of total worldwide annual turnover**, whichever
   is higher, for prohibited-practice violations (lower tiers for other breaches).
5. **Sector map (US):** healthcare → **HIPAA/HITECH** (PHI); finance/fintech → **GLBA, BSA/AML, KYC,
   PCI DSS, NYDFS 23 NYCRR 500**; privacy → **GDPR (EU), CCPA/CPRA (CA)**. Each fixed-panel industry maps
   to a distinct regulatory bundle (regulated vs. light columns in the B12 panel).
