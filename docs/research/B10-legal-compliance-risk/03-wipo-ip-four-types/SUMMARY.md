## Key claims (with locators, exact quotes)
1. **WIPO definition (verbatim):** *"Intellectual property (IP) refers to creations of the mind, such
   as inventions; literary and artistic works; designs; and symbols, names and images used in commerce."*
2. **WIPO recognizes six IP categories:** **Patents, Copyright, Trademarks, Industrial Designs,
   Geographical Indications, and Trade Secrets.** (US business practice commonly emphasizes the **four**
   core types: patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets.)
3. **Patents** (PTRC; 35 U.S.C. §101): protect **new, useful, and non-obvious** inventions —
   processes, machines, manufactures, or compositions of matter, or improvements thereof.
4. **Copyright** (PTRC; 17 U.S.C. §102): protects **original works of authorship** fixed in a tangible
   medium (literary, dramatic, musical, artistic). **Subsists automatically from the moment of
   creation/fixation** — registration is not required for the right to exist (though it confers
   enforcement benefits).
5. **Trademarks** (PTRC; Lanham Act): protect **distinctive words, phrases, logos, symbols, slogans**
   used to **identify and distinguish** goods/services in the marketplace.
6. **Trade secrets** (PTRC; see DTSA, source 04): protect **confidential, commercially valuable
   information** known to a limited group and subject to **reasonable steps to keep it secret.**
