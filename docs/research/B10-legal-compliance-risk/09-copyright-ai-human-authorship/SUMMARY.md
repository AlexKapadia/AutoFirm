## Key claims (with locators, exact quotes)
1. **Human authorship is required** (Thaler v. Perlmutter, D.D.C. 2023, aff'd D.C. Cir. 2025): *"human
   authorship is an essential part of a valid copyright claim."* Works authored **autonomously by AI
   with no human author are NOT copyrightable.**
2. **Creative-control test** (Copyright Office 2023 Guidance / 2025 Part 2 report, verbatim): *"what
   matters is the extent to which the human had creative control over the work's expression"*; where AI
   *"determines the expressive elements of its output, the generated material is not the product of human
   authorship."*
3. **Disclaimer duty** (2023 Guidance): applicants must **identify and disclaim AI-generated portions**
   and may claim copyright **"only for their own [human] contributions."**
4. **Assistive AI is fine** (2025 Part 2 report): AI used as a **tool** that assists human creation does
   not defeat copyright; the work must reflect **human creative input** in its expressive choices.
5. **Settled at the top** (Skadden, Morgan Lewis): the D.C. Circuit affirmance (Mar 18, 2025) and the
   subsequent cert. denial cement that **only human beings can be copyright authors** under current US law.
