## Key claims (with locators)
1. **The IRS recognizes these primary federal business structures** (IRS *Business Structures*):
   **Sole Proprietorship, Partnership, Corporation, S Corporation, and Limited Liability Company (LLC).**
2. **LLC tax classification is elective** (IRS *LLC Filing as a Corporation or Partnership*; *Single
   Member LLCs*): a single-member LLC defaults to a **disregarded entity** (sole-proprietor tax
   treatment); a multi-member LLC defaults to **partnership** taxation; an LLC may **elect corporate
   taxation** (Form 8832) and may further **elect S-corporation status** (Form 2553).
   Exact distinction: an LLC is a *legal entity type*; an S-Corp is a *tax election*.
3. **Limited liability** (SBA *Choose a Business Structure*): LLCs and corporations create a separate
   legal entity; owner liability is generally **limited to business assets** — personal assets are
   shielded absent veil-piercing. Sole proprietorships and general partnerships do **not** provide
   this shield.
4. **Pass-through vs. double taxation:** S-corps, partnerships, and default-classified LLCs are
   **pass-through** (income taxed once, on owners' returns); **C-corporations face double taxation**
   (entity-level corporate tax + shareholder-level tax on dividends).
5. The SBA frames entity choice as driven by **(a) liability, (b) taxation, (c) ability to raise
   capital, (d) recordkeeping/administrative burden** — the four decision axes.

## Corroboration (independent, for the liability + tax-treatment claims)
- IRS *Business Structures* + SBA *Choose a Business Structure* (two independent US federal agencies)
  agree on the entity menu and the liability/tax axes. A practitioner secondary (NCSU Poole College

  corroborates the elective-classification mechanics — used for color only, not a relied-upon basis.
