## Key claims (with locators)
1. **Federal civil remedy** (DTSA §2; 18 U.S.C. §1836(b)(1)): the DTSA **creates a federal private
   civil cause of action** for trade-secret misappropriation where the secret **relates to a product or
   service used in, or intended for use in, interstate or foreign commerce.** (Before 2016, trade-secret
   law was state-law-only under the UTSA.)
2. **"Misappropriation"** (18 U.S.C. §1839(5)): **(A) acquisition** of a trade secret by a person who
   knows or has reason to know it was acquired by **improper means**; OR **(B) disclosure or use**
   without consent by one who used improper means or knew it was derived from improper means.
3. **"Improper means"** (18 U.S.C. §1839(6)): includes theft, bribery, misrepresentation, breach/
   inducement of breach of a duty to maintain secrecy, and espionage — **but does NOT include reverse
   engineering, independent derivation, or any other lawful means of acquisition.**
4. **"Trade secret" definition** (18 U.S.C. §1839(3)): information the owner has taken **reasonable
   measures to keep secret** AND which derives **independent economic value** from not being generally
   known to, or readily ascertainable by, another person who can obtain economic value from its
   disclosure or use. (DTSA harmonized this with the UTSA standard.)
5. **Remedies** (18 U.S.C. §1836(b)(3)): injunctions, damages (actual loss + unjust enrichment, or a
   reasonable royalty), **exemplary damages up to 2x** for willful/malicious misappropriation, and
   attorney's fees; plus an **ex parte civil seizure** in extraordinary circumstances.
