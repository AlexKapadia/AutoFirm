## Key claims (with locators)
1. **Common-law contract elements** (Saylor *Formation*; corroborated by Jenkins §I): a contract
   requires **(1) offer, (2) acceptance, (3) consideration**, plus a **legal purpose** and parties with
   **capacity**. Offer + acceptance together = **mutual assent**.
2. **Mirror-image rule (common law):** acceptance must **exactly match** the offer's terms; a variant
   reply is a **counter-offer**, not acceptance (Jenkins, common-law section).
3. **UCC §2-207 displaces the mirror-image rule** for sales of goods: an acceptance with **additional
   or different terms can still form a contract** between merchants; the extra terms become part of the
   contract unless they materially alter it / are objected to (UCC §2-207; Jenkins).
4. **UCC §2-204 definiteness:** a goods contract needs only the **good and the quantity**; missing
   terms are filled by **UCC gap-filler default rules** (price, delivery, time). Common law by contrast
   requires reasonable definiteness as to **all material terms**.
5. **UCC §2-205 firm offer:** a **merchant's signed written offer** to hold an offer open is
   **irrevocable for the stated period, up to 3 months (90 days), without consideration** — an
   exception to the common-law rule that an offer is freely revocable before acceptance.
6. **Governing-law split:** the **UCC governs contracts for the sale of goods**; **common law governs
   services and everything not under the UCC.** All 50 states have adopted some version of the UCC.
