## Core responsibilities
Investor research & matching; pitch deck creation; financial model/projections; data room prep & management;
investor outreach & follow-up; CRM for the raise; investor updates/reporting; cap table; due-diligence Q&A.

## Automation state of the art
- **Investor matching:** ML-based tools match startups to relevant investors to improve fit (Evalyze).
- **Pitch decks:** Storydoc AI deck builder generates structured outlines from a one-paragraph brief (2024);
  Alai added a content-quality check flagging underdeveloped slides (2025). AI speeds deck creation 8-10x.
- **Outreach/CRM:** outreach platforms automate sequences and follow-ups, with native HubSpot/Salesforce
  integration to track the raise; teams support investor mapping, data rooms, and materials.
- **Caution (cited):** in 2024-2025 scrutiny on business models intensified, and **investors spot generic AI
  output instantly** — automation accelerates production but does not substitute for substance/credibility.

## Scaling 1-person -> enterprise
- **1-person (startup founder):** highest-value use — deck drafting, investor matching, outreach sequencing,
  data-room assembly; founder owns the narrative and the conversations.
- **Growth-stage:** dedicated IR-style workflows, investor-update automation, structured data rooms.
- **Enterprise/public:** investor relations becomes regulated disclosure (earnings, filings) — heavily human
  + compliance-gated; automation limited to drafting and scheduling.

## Human-in-the-loop
The pitch narrative, investor conversations, valuation/terms, and any public/regulated disclosure are human.
Production (decks, matching, outreach logistics, data-room assembly) automates well.
